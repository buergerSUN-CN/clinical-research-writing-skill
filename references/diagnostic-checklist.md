# Full Diagnostic Checklist — "Too Basic-Science?"

Run this on a draft (or a single section) to catch basic-science writing and convert it to clinical voice. Each item is a yes/no check; a 'no' points to the fix. Items are grounded in the 14-paper corpus (paperNN tags show where the pattern is evidenced). Not every item applies to every study — the parentheticals tell you when an item does NOT apply.

Study-type shorthand: **interventional/cohort/prognostic** (paper01-08,10-14), **diagnostic-accuracy** (paper09), **imaging-technique** (paper09; image quality as primary endpoint), **procedural-comparative** (paper01).

1. Does the FIRST sentence of the Introduction state disease burden, epidemiology, the current standard of care/clinical dilemma, the RESIDUAL burden despite proven treatment (~50% not independent after EVT), or a limitation of current standard-of-care imaging (DSA invasive; CTA blooming; manual volumetry slow; software-dependent metrics unavailable) — rather than a molecular/mechanistic knowledge gap? (If it opens on a pathway or biology-for-its-own-sake, it reads too basic-science.)

2. Is the stated AIM a clinical decision, prognostic, or diagnostic question ('which patients', 'predict [endpoint]', 'safety and effectiveness', 'the prognostic/diagnostic value of [marker] for [endpoint]', 'diagnostic performance vs [reference standard]')? Note: 'to investigate/assess/explore/determine/evaluate the value of [marker]' is clinical voice — only 'to investigate the MECHANISM/biological role of' is the red flag. For Eur Radiol/imaging, the bare-infinitive Objective is expected, not a defect.

3. Is the gap framed as a clinical/evidence/practice gap ('studies are scarce', 'remains unclear', 'not fully explored', 'still not clear until now', 'no publications have reported', 'is urgently needed', 'has not yet been reported') rather than as 'the mechanism is unknown'?

4. Are the primary outcomes HARD, patient-centered clinical endpoints (mRS 0-2/0-1 at 90 d, NIHSS, sICH/HT, mortality, recurrent stroke, mMCAi, technical/clinical success, complications, length of stay) rather than surrogate biological readouts? For an imaging-PROGNOSTIC marker, is it validated AGAINST a clinical endpoint or a named reference standard? (For an imaging-TECHNIQUE paper, a quantitative image-quality / diagnostic-confidence PRIMARY endpoint, e.g. paper09, is legitimate and need not be a clinical outcome.)

5. For a DIAGNOSTIC-ACCURACY paper: is there an explicit INDEX TEST and a named REFERENCE STANDARD (e.g. DSA; follow-up DWI/FIV; ASL), and is the reference-standard strategy justified (e.g. 2-tier to minimize work-up bias, DSA only when clinically indicated)? Are sensitivity/specificity/PPV/NPV/accuracy reported as RAW numerators/denominators, head-to-head against the comparator — and NOT dressed up with an ROC/Youden/AUC the binary read does not have? Is the reporting guideline STROBE (the corpus exemplar), NOT a fabricated STARD statement?

6. Does the Results section lead with an absolute event rate/prevalence WITH a denominator (e.g. 48/395, 12.2%; GIC in 18/115, 15.7%) before any inferential statistic — OR, for a procedural-comparative paper, lead with technical/clinical success and procedural-time/patient-experience contrasts (%/median + P) — OR, for an imaging-technique paper, lead with the prespecified image-quality/diagnostic-confidence primary endpoint?

7. Is every key association reported with an effect size AND 95% CI AND exact P (OR/HR/aOR with CI, or 'X vs. Y, P=...'), not just a P value or a fold-change? (Pure procedural-comparative outcomes may use group %/median + exact P without an OR/CI.)

8. If a PROGNOSTIC marker is involved, is it translated into a bedside-usable cut-off via ROC (Youden cut-off, AUC with 95% CI, sensitivity, specificity)? When two prognostic markers/models are ranked, is the AUC comparison tested with the NAMED DeLong test (paper12/13)? Do NOT assume DeLong is universal — paper14 compares nested AUCs (p=0.045) without naming it. Is Hosmer-Lemeshow calibration reported ONLY where a single combined prediction model is the deliverable (paper10), not bolted onto every model (paper13/14 report combined-model AUC without calibration)?

9. When two imaging markers are CORRELATED, did the paper run a collinearity diagnostic (VIF) and apply the remedy — split into PARALLEL multivariable models (paper12) or switch to STEPWISE selection (paper11, paper14) — rather than entering both into one unstable model? Where two measures are argued to be surrogates, is a Spearman/Pearson correlation coefficient reported WITH a slight/fair/moderate/good/excellent band (paper11/12)? Is method-comparison against the reference visualized with a Bland-Altman mean difference + LoA where relevant (paper11)?

10. For ANY subjective imaging/event read (sICH, restenosis, FVH/GIC presence, ASPECTS, perfusion maps, image quality): are the reads BLINDED, independent, by stated-experience radiologists with consensus/third-reader adjudication, AND is INTER-READER reproducibility reported (weighted Cohen kappa or ICC with 95% CI, WITH a poor/fair/good/excellent band)? (Blinded adjudication and reproducibility are appropriately absent for objective procedural-metric or administrative-mortality endpoints — do not flag them there.)

11. For a paired index-test-vs-comparator design, is the SAME-PATIENT paired comparison described with reading-order controls (comparator read first, washout period to minimize recall bias, randomized case order) driving within-subject paired Wilcoxon/paired-t/McNemar testing (paper09)?

12. Is the cohort described in the right terms for its design — 'consecutive patients' + named center for single-center registries/imaging cohorts (paper02/03/09/11/12/13/14); the randomized/multicenter parent-trial cohort + registration number for post-hoc RCT analyses (paper04/06/10); a named multicenter cohort otherwise (paper08) — with enrollment window, numbered inclusion/exclusion criteria (incl. required imaging availability), IRB/Helsinki, consent/waiver, and a CONSORT-style flow? (Do not flag absence of 'consecutive' in post-hoc-RCT/multicenter papers. Note the Eur Radiol Methodology block may self-label 'case-control study' even for a consecutive single-center retrospective cohort — paper11-14.)

13. Are outcomes/disease terms anchored to recognized clinical classifications/validated scales and their cut-offs (Heidelberg Bleeding Classification, NASCET/WASID, TICI/mTICI/eTICI with mTICI 2b-3 = success, Hunt-Hess, modified Fisher, mRS dichotomized good/poor, NIHSS, ASPECTS, ISR = ≥50%)?

14. If groups are compared, is confounding controlled (PSM or multivariable adjustment) with covariates named and balance diagnostics (SMD/variance ratio) reported? For case-control imaging studies, is a univariate screen feeding multivariable (or stepwise, when collinear) regression specified?

15. Is a patient-flow accounting present (screened -> excluded with reasons -> analyzed), and are null/non-significant findings reported explicitly rather than buried? Is any safety/harm signal reported co-equally with efficacy, with radiation dose (CTDIvol/DLP) reported separately for CT procedures (not conflated into the 'without adverse events' sentence)?

16. In the Discussion, is mechanism/pathophysiology kept SUBORDINATE — invoked only as supportive plausibility (coagulopathy, no-reflow, BBB disruption, blooming artifact, pH normalization) — while an explicit clinical-implications/management paragraph carries the contribution? For imaging papers, does it explain WHY the prior approach failed clinically, and reframe any honest quantitative trade-off (e.g. lower SNR/CNR) as the expected price of resolution/interpretability rather than a defect?

17. Are the findings benchmarked against NAMED prior clinical series, registries, or RCTs, with discrepancies reconciled by study setting (RCT rigor, single- vs multicenter, protocol/threshold, patient constitution)?

18. Does the Discussion/Conclusion translate the finding into a concrete MANAGEMENT action (surveillance interval, patient selection, perioperative timing, first-line ranking, early identification of high-risk patients) OR a scan-interpretation instruction ('CTP may overestimate core'; 'do not exclude from MT on CTP alone'; 'initiate rehab if FVH present') rather than into 'biological understanding'? Is the marker's real-world practicality stated ('simple, accessible, reproducible'; 'more practical for real clinical settings')? Where a subgroup re-derives a threshold by onset time or exposes futile recanalization (paper11/14), is that surfaced as the clinical message?

19. Are Limitations enumerated pragmatically (single-center/retrospective/post-hoc, small events/cohort, single-ethnicity generalizability), each tied to a clinical consequence, with named biases AND their DIRECTION — especially partial-verification/work-up bias inflating specificity/NPV for diagnostic-accuracy studies, the surrogate-comparator-reconstructed-from-same-raw-data caveat limiting superiority claims, and MRI-tolerance selection bias (severe/HT patients excluded) for imaging-prognostic studies — plus a call for prospective/external/RCT confirmation?

20. Is the language measured and hedged ('may', 'might', 'could', 'appear to', 'suggest') with at most ONE restrained 'first study' claim — and free of mechanism-novelty hype? Does a diagnostic paper avoid claiming superiority beyond what the reference-standard/surrogate-comparator design supports?

21. Does the Title name the clinical endpoint/predictor/comparison and the population ('Predictors of...', 'Prognostic value of [marker] for [outcome] after [procedure]', 'X predicts Y', 'X Versus Y', 'for Noninvasive Detection of [entity]') — rather than foregrounding the technology/physics/molecule alone? (A study-design subtitle is a frequent but OPTIONAL flag.) Does the Abstract Conclusion deliver an actionable, hedged recommendation and populate the CORRECT journal box — Eur Radiol bulleted 'Key Points' (papers 07, 10-14) plus 'Clinical relevance' (paper07); JAHA 'What is new / Clinical implications' — WITHOUT inventing a Key Points box for a Stroke/AHA diagnostic paper (paper09 has none)?
