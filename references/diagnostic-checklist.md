# Full Diagnostic Checklist — "Too Basic-Science?"

Distilled from 21 exemplar papers — a Nanjing neurovascular/stroke + neuroimaging group (single-centre observational, imaging/diagnostic-accuracy) plus landmark multicentre RCTs (NEJM/Lancet/JAMA, basilar-artery-occlusion thrombectomy). Every exemplar is quoted verbatim, tagged with its source paperNN; three genres are distinguished throughout.

Run on a draft (or one section) to catch basic-science writing. Each item is a yes/no check; parentheticals say when it does NOT apply by genre. Now includes figure-legend, table-title/footnote, and concision checks.

1. TITLE LEADS WITH POPULATION + DECISION/COMPARISON/PREDICTOR, NOT A MECHANISM/MOLECULE/DEVICE/METHOD WORD — Does the first content word name a clinical-utility concept (outcome, effectiveness, prognostic value, predictors, treatment) and does the title foreground the named patient population AND the decision/comparison/predictor, with any study-design subtitle as an OPTIONAL credibility tail? FAIL: a method/statistic/metric word in the front slot ('spatial resolution', 'kernel', 'phantom', 'image quality', 'machine learning', 'glycoprotein IIb/IIIa', 'platelet aggregation', 'oxygenation', 'penumbra', 'neuroprotection') or a title centered on device physics/a molecule with no endpoint/population. paper01 'Comparing Transbrachial and Transradial as Alternatives to Transfemoral Access for Large-Bore Neuro Stenting: Insights From a Propensity-Matched Study' [paper01]; paper02 'Emergency admission plasma D-dimer: a novel predictor for symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage after thrombectomy in acute ischemic stroke' [paper02]; paper03 'Incidence and predictors of restenosis following successful recanalization of non-acute internal carotid artery occlusion in 252 cases' [paper03]; paper04 utility-framed marker+decision+population+provenance '...Potential of BMI as a screening indicator for extracranial-intracranial bypass surgery... a post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial' [paper04]; paper05 names both clinical entities + confounding-control method 'Diffuse Angiogram-Negative Subarachnoid Hemorrhage Versus Aneurysmal Subarachnoid Hemorrhage: A Propensity Score-Matched Analysis' [paper05]; paper06 'A Post Hoc Analysis' placed in the title to pre-commit a hypothesis-generating frame [paper06]; paper08 leads with procedure then names the exact decision population then appends design 'Thrombectomy in Stroke Patients with Large Vessel Occlusion and Mild Symptoms: Insights from a Multicenter Observational Study' [paper08]; paper09 leads on the clinical action 'Detection' with patient-benefit adjective 'Noninvasive' and NO 'spatial resolution/kernel/phantom/image quality' [paper09]; paper11 '[Predictors of] ghost infarct core... in stroke patients with successful recanalization after MT' (no method/algorithm word) [paper11]; paper12 leads 'Prognostic value of ASPECTS...' [paper12]; paper14 'ASPECTS-based net water uptake predicts poor reperfusion and poor clinical outcomes in patients with ischemic stroke' (biomarker tied to its two clinical payoffs + population) [paper14]; paper19 'Endovascular Therapy versus Medical Management for Basilar Artery Occlusion Presenting beyond 24 Hours' (no method/statistic/metric) [paper19]; paper21 'Trial of Endovascular Therapy for Acute Ischemic Stroke with Large Infarct' (patient+intervention, zero mechanism/device/statistic/result) [paper21]; paper22 'Endovascular Treatment of Medium-Vessel-Occlusion Strokes' (decision-framed 'Treatment', no mechanistic verb) [paper22]; paper25 mechanism words excluded from title, receptor pharmacology deferred to Abstract, title outcome 'First-Pass Recanalization' is a decision-relevant procedural endpoint not a biomarker [paper25]; paper26 opens with outcome concept 'Effectiveness...' and pins the exact stratum 'Acute Mild' [paper26]. (For a technique/imaging-optimization study a plain parameter-describing noun-phrase is acceptable IF the patient population is the closing anchor — paper07 'Effect of saturation pulse power on chemical exchange saturation transfer imaging in patients with acute ischemic stroke'; a bare phantom/physics/animal title with no patient population FAILS. A study-design subtitle is OPTIONAL but a frequent credibility flag; for landmark RCTs it doubles as evidence-pedigree signaling, e.g. paper20 'A Secondary Analysis of a Randomized Clinical Trial'. For an evidence-synthesis the design may be a descriptive colon tail, paper23 '...: individual participant data meta-analysis of six randomised trials'.)

2. TITLE QUALIFIER NAMES THE CLINICAL/DECISION-BOUNDARY GAP, NOT A BIOLOGICAL HYPOTHESIS — Does any title qualifier state the off-guideline / decision-boundary scenario (a time window, a previously-excluded or under-studied population) in patient terms? paper19 names the off-guideline gap as a time window '...beyond 24 Hours' [paper19]; paper21 population defined by the very feature that makes the question clinically open 'Large Infarct' [paper21]; paper26 pins 'Acute Mild' to the BAO population so the title signals a narrow, unresolved sub-question [paper26]. FAIL: a qualifier naming a pathway/mechanism. (DOES NOT APPLY to a pure imaging-biomarker title, which carries the clinical payoff instead of a decision-boundary qualifier — paper14; or to an unstratified whole-population study by design.)

3. ABSTRACT/INTRODUCTION OPENER LEADS ON DISEASE BURDEN / STANDARD-OF-CARE LIMITATION / CLINICAL UNCERTAINTY — NOT BIOLOGY OR PHYSICS — Does the first 1-2 sentences lead with disease burden / standard-of-care default / residual-risk-despite-treatment / equipoise, with mechanism demoted to a subordinate clause? FAIL: an opener centered on a pathway/molecule/signal-physics for its own sake. paper01 opens on the standard of care and its limitation 'Traditionally, transfemoral access (TFA) has been the primary choice... However, the expansion of indications... spurred a demand for a wider selection of access.' [paper01]; paper02 opens on complication burden + patient cost 'Hemorrhagic transformation (HT), especially symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage (sICH), is a common and fatal complication... which decreases or counteracts the benefit of thrombectomy.' [paper02]; paper03 epidemiology + patient consequence 'Atherosclerotic internal carotid artery occlusion (ICAO) contributes to approximately 10% of TIAs and 15-25% of ischemic strokes...' [paper03]; paper04 burden + residual-risk pivot 'Intracranial atherosclerotic disease is a leading cause of ischemic stroke worldwide...' then 'Despite intensive medical management... face a stroke recurrence risk exceeding 10% annually' [paper04]; paper05 'Angiogram-negative subarachnoid hemorrhage... accounts for approximately 10% to 15% of all cases of SAH' [paper05]; paper06 'Intracranial atherosclerotic disease is a prevalent cause of ischemic stroke globally and is associated with a significant risk of recurrent strokes' (framed around patients, never biology) [paper06]; paper07 'The global incidence of acute ischemic stroke (AIS) is increasing and remains associated with high rates of disability and mortality' (physics kept in the middle, subordinated) [paper07]; paper09 first clause is the gap with physics demoted 'Noninvasive detection of neurovascular in-stent restenosis (ISR) remains challenging because of blooming artifacts and limited spatial resolution on conventional computed tomography angiography (CTA).' [paper09]; paper11 opens at clinical decision-making 'Quantification of perfusion profile, especially the volume of infarct core (IC), was very important for establishing the individualized treatment plan...' [paper11]; paper12 opens on residual patient-outcome burden '...the rate of functional independence after EVT was only about 50%' [paper12]; paper14 'Reperfusion therapies... are beneficial for salvaging penumbral tissue... in patients with acute ischemic stroke' (what therapy DOES, not mechanism) [paper14]; paper22 disease burden + 'important clinical challenge' in the first sentence [paper22]; paper24 concedes the standard works then pivots 'even with endovascular treatment, approximately half of patients do not have a favourable functional outcome.' [paper24]; paper25 mechanism stated once then pivots to 'the role of tirofiban is uncertain' [paper25]; paper26 'Basilar artery occlusion (BAO) represents ~5% to 10% of all acute large vessel occlusion stroke... the mortality rate of patients with untreated BAO can reach up to 85% to 95%.' [paper26]; landmark RCTs build the funnel from the named-trial/guideline landscape — paper19 (DAWN, DEFUSE 3, MR CLEAN LATE), paper21 limitation-of-current-guidelines funnel. (Applies to all genres; an opener leading with pathway/molecule/signal-physics FAILS even for a technique paper.)

4. AIM/OBJECTIVE IS A DECISION / PREDICTION / SELECTION QUESTION, PATIENT-FIRST, MIRRORING TITLE & CONCLUSION — Is the aim a clinical comparison, prediction, patient-selection, effectiveness, or diagnostic-performance question (action stem / infinitive: 'to compare ... versus ... in patients with ...', 'to explore the ability to predict', 'to evaluate the utility as a screening tool', 'to assess the prognostic value'), NOT 'investigate the MECHANISM/biological role of ...'? paper01 'we aimed to compare the outcomes of patients undergoing TBA, TRA, and TFA for large-bore neuro stenting...' [paper01]; paper02 'To explore the ability of admission plasma D-dimer levels to predict sICH after thrombectomy.' [paper02]; paper03 predictor + tool aim ending in decision-making [paper03]; paper04 'We also assessed BMI's potential as a marker to identify those who might benefit from bypass surgery...' [paper04]; paper05 'to investigate whether the clinical complications and functional outcomes differed between patients with dan-SAH versus aSAH' (patient-first hypothesis test) [paper05]; paper08 'to compare the efficacy of EVT in combination with BMM versus BMM alone' [paper08]; paper09 'to evaluate the diagnostic performance of UHR PCD-CTA for the assessment of ISR... compared with DSA and simulated EID-CTA.' (the stated Objective; note its PRESPECIFIED primary endpoint and sample-size basis are the subjective image-quality scores, with ISR diagnostic accuracy co-reported) [paper09]; paper12 numbered decision-relevant aims '...whether post-treatment DWI ASPECTS could serve as a surrogate of infarction volume...' [paper12]; paper19 aim mirrors Purpose ('To compare ... versus ... in patients with ...') [paper19]; paper26 hedged decision verb 'aimed to explore the efficacy of EVT in acute BAO with a low NIHSS.' [paper26]. FAIL: 'to investigate the MECHANISM/biological role'. (For a technique study the aim may be DUAL — a technical aim TWINNED with a prognostic/clinical-relevance aim, paper07 'to determine the optimal saturation power... Furthermore, we assessed the prognostic value...'; a purely technical aim with no clinical twin FAILS. RCTs/effectiveness studies state an aim/superiority hypothesis rather than a hedged predictive hypothesis; prognostic studies (paper14) may add a hedged predictive hypothesis 'we hypothesized that... could predict...'.)

5. GAP IS A CLINICAL / EVIDENCE / POPULATION GAP, NOT 'THE MECHANISM IS UNKNOWN' — Is the unmet need framed with decision-grade words ('scarce / not analyzed / no publications have reported / lacking / sparse / remains unclear / remains uncertain / has not been well delineated / no RCT evidence / reliably'), and as which patients/subgroup were/weren't studied — NOT 'understudied / poorly understood / the mechanism is unknown'? paper01 literature-concession chain 'Currently, TBA is not recommended as a primary access site due to limited literature on TBA neurointervention.' [paper01]; paper02 'no publications have reported the relationship between D-dimer levels and sICH of patients with AIS after EVT.' [paper02]; paper03 'studies specifically addressing this issue are lacking, and the factors associated with restenosis post-ICAO recanalization remain unexplored.' [paper03]; paper04 'research targeting specific groups... remains sparse.' [paper04]; paper09 'to our knowledge, in vivo evaluation of ISR in neurovascular stents using UHR PCD-CTA has not yet been reported.' [paper09]; paper11 'few studies focusing on GIC using follow-up DWI as the reference standard had been reported until now.' [paper11]; paper14 decision-grade word 'reliably' '...studies are still needed to reliably investigate the relationship...' [paper14]; paper19 'the safety and effectiveness of EVT in patients with BAO treated beyond 24 hours remains unclear' and 'there is no randomized controlled trial evidence...' [paper19]; paper20 'the long-term clinical outcomes of these patients have not been well delineated' [paper20]; paper21 'Whether endovascular therapy benefits patients with a large infarct core remains uncertain.' [paper21]; paper22 population critique — gap defined by which patients were studied [paper22]; paper25 evidentiary gap 'prior benefit (RESCUE BT) was only a post hoc finding, never a prespecified primary endpoint.' [paper25]; paper26 'these patients were excluded from previous studies or only accounted for a small subgroup, leaving the efficacy... uncertain.' [paper26]; paper23 flat absolute 'No randomised controlled trial has established a benefit of...' [paper23]. (Applies to all genres. Where mechanism appears in the Introduction it must be subordinated and immediately translated into a management/procedural consequence — paper23 lytic dwell times -> cost/workflow; paper25 mechanism -> fewer reocclusions -> fewer passes.)

6. PRIMARY OUTCOME IS A HARD PATIENT-CENTERED CLINICAL EVENT / FUNCTIONAL STATE AT A NAMED TIMEPOINT — NOT A SURROGATE/BIOLOGICAL READOUT — Is the headline outcome a patient-relevant event/function (mRS band, functional independence, mortality, rehemorrhage, DCI, sICH) or an operationally-defined technical/clinical success rate, at a standard timepoint (90 d / 3 months / 30 days), NOT reperfusion grade/AUC/a radiographic surrogate dressed as the main result? paper01 safety composite by patient harms 'Clinical success was defined as the absence of perioperative mortality, intracranial hemorrhage, disabling stroke, and other events resulting in new-onset functional impairment...' [paper01]; paper02 outcome in patient-affecting terms 'symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage' [paper02]; paper03 links surrogate (restenosis) to hard outcome 'The incidence of stroke was higher in patients with restenosis (25.0% vs 1.5%, P<0.01).' [paper03]; paper04 trial composite anchored to clinical events (any stroke/death within 30 d, or IIS to 2 y) [paper04]; paper05 every endpoint is a clinical event at a clinical timepoint (rehemorrhage, in-hospital mortality, DCI, 3-month mRS) [paper05]; paper08 '3-month modified Rankin Scale (excellent: mRS 0-1, functional independence: mRS 0-2)' [paper08]; paper19 primary outcome 'a patient-meaningful functional state (mRS 0-3), not a surrogate/imaging endpoint' [paper19]; paper20 mRS as primary, never angiographic/radiographic surrogate [paper20]; paper21 endpoints throughout are disability (mRS) and bleeding [paper21]; paper22 'mRS shift... no surrogate/mechanistic primary endpoint' [paper22]; paper25 JOINT efficacy-without-harm composite 'first-pass recanalization without intracranial hemorrhagic risk' (posed the way a clinician weighs the decision) [paper25]; paper26 'functional independence, defined as mRS score of <=2 at 90 days' [paper26]. FAIL: a biomarker/imaging surrogate reported as the endpoint with no link to a clinical outcome. (Genre split for imaging studies: for an imaging-PROGNOSTIC/diagnostic study the legitimate metric is the index test's discrimination, but the endpoint being PREDICTED is still the patient outcome — paper14 reports AUC but the predicted endpoint is mRS>2 at 90 days. paper09 is an imaging-TECHNIQUE study: its PRESPECIFIED PRIMARY endpoint and sample-size basis are the subjective image-quality scores — 'The sample size was determined based on the primary end point of subjective image quality scores.' — while diagnostic accuracy for ISR against DSA is co-reported, not the prespecified primary; a technique study must still TWIN its technical deliverable with a named patient endpoint, paper07 pairs 'optimal saturation power' with '90-day neurological outcomes'. A paper whose ONLY endpoint is a bare signal/physics metric with no clinical twin FAILS the clinical test.)

7. KEY POINTS / SUMMARY / IN-CONTEXT BOX IS GENRE-CORRECT, DECISION-FIRST, AND HEDGED — Where the journal mandates one, does the box exist, is every line stand-alone and patient/value-led in plain language with denominator-anchored numbers, is mechanism absent, and is novelty restraint-hedged ('To our knowledge ... first ... completed', not bare 'first-ever')? paper02 (JNIS What is already known / What this study adds / How this study might affect...) third box on workflow 'D-dimer levels, which are reported within half an hour and require no additional calculations, are more suitable for use in emergency situations...' [paper02]; paper03 (BMJ three boxes) third box on decision-making [paper03]; paper04 (IJS Highlights, 3 plain-language bullets) bullet 3 'BMI levels have the potential to identify patients... who might be candidates for bypass surgery.' [paper04]; paper14 'Key Points' compresses each finding into a stand-alone patient-value-led one-liner [paper14]; paper20 Key Points triad with hedged 'suggest' Meaning line [paper20]; paper23 Research-in-context quantifies the population ('around 50%'), names real-world pressures (drug shortages), single narrowly-scoped 'first' claim [paper23]; paper24 novelty 'To our knowledge... first multicentre, large trial completed', never bare 'first-ever' [paper24]; paper25 Key Points Question/Findings/Meaning, purely clinical, mechanism absent [paper25]. FAIL: a box that restates method, is mechanism-led, or hypes novelty. (DOES NOT APPLY where the journal supplies no box — NEJM paper21 'no separate Key Points box', the structured headers carry the framing; Acad Radiol paper01 has NO Key Points/Clinical-relevance box, so do NOT invent one — its Abstract uses the 'Rationale and Objectives' header and lands on a hedged recommendation 'Procedural delays in TRA may favor TBA as the first-line alternative access to TFA.' [paper01]. Match the journal's actual box.)

8. ABSTRACT CONCLUSION / FINAL CONCLUSION LANDS ON AN ACTIONABLE, HEDGED, POPULATION-BOUNDED MANAGEMENT STATEMENT — Does the conclusion translate the finding into a specific clinical action (first-line ranking, surveillance interval, patient selection, perioperative decision, surrogate utility, do-not-exclude rule) scoped to 'this cohort of patients with [population]', hedged with a modal (may/might/could/potential/promising), with NO new content, NO mechanism, NO 'first-ever'/novelty hype, and — for treatment studies — the benefit never reported without its paired harm/safety verdict? paper01 first-line ranking 'Procedural delays in TRA may favor TBA as the first-line alternative access to TFA.' [paper01]; paper02 perioperative-action triple 'limiting the number of thrombectomy attempts, determining the timing of procedure termination, and guiding the use of antithrombotic/anticoagulant agents.' [paper02]; paper03 surveillance interval + mirror low-risk guidance 'every 3 months, especially within the first 15 months post-recanalization for high-risk individuals... the follow-up interval for patients without restenosis predictors may be extended.' [paper03]; paper04 patient selection 'integrating BMI levels with traditional hemodynamic indicators could enhance the screening strategy...' [paper04]; paper05 'compared with patients with dan-SAH, patients with aSAH were associated with significantly higher rehemorrhage rates...' + forward hook 'may help clinicians to carry out targeted treatment according to patient's hemorrhage subtype' [paper05]; paper06 do-not-under-treat directive, no novelty hype, no 'further mechanistic work needed' tail [paper06]; paper08 guarded guideline-style verdict — no overall advantage with a named candidate subgroup carved out [paper08]; paper09 'may serve as a promising tool for poststent surveillance.' [paper09]; paper11 bedside directive 'Patients cannot be excluded from MT solely based on baseline CTP-derived ischemic core volume, especially for patients with a shorter onset time.' [paper11]; paper12 'might be a potential surrogate of infarction volume...' [paper12]; paper22 signature 'but also' trade-off + double hedge 'support the consideration of endovascular therapy in carefully selected patients' [paper22]; paper24 'states direction of benefit, pairs it with a safety reassurance (without raising safety concerns)' [paper24]; paper25 actor-directed hedge 'neurointerventionalists may consider...' [paper25]; paper26 three-part benefit-harm-reassurance ledger 'EVT is associated with an increased likelihood of 90-day functional independence... EVT could increase the risk of sICH, but this does not contribute to a higher mortality. The findings should be further validated in randomized trials...' bounded to 'In this cohort of patients with acute mild BAO...' [paper26]. FAIL: a conclusion ending on mechanism/'first-ever', or omitting the harm coordinate, or generalizing beyond the sample. (For definitive RCTs the verdict may be firmer and need no deferral-to-future-RCTs; for observational/post-hoc work soften further to 'may favor'/'may consider' in proportion to the evidence.)

9. EVERY EVENT RATE CARRIES ITS NUMERATOR/DENOMINATOR n/N (%) BEFORE THE PERCENTAGE, WITH THE PRIMARY EVENT RATE STATED BEFORE INFERENTIAL STATS — Does every rate/column-header show n/N (%) so small absolute counts are visible, with denominators re-stated when the analysis population changes, and is the absolute primary event count led before any model output? FAIL: a bare percentage or denominator-free count. paper01 every rate carries n/N 'the TFA group predominantly employed vascular closure devices (95.7%, 88/92)' [paper01]; paper02 leads with the primary event count 'Forty-eight (12.2%) patients demonstrated sICH...' [paper02]; paper03 'restenosis was detected in 56 patients (22.2%)' [paper03]; paper04 'IIS occurred in 16 (17.4%) of the 92 patients with BMI >=24.5 kg/m2, but in none (0%) of the 73 patients with BMI <24.5 kg/m2' [paper04]; paper05 running percentages with denominators '878 (83.1%) were identified as aSAH, and 148 (14.0%) as an-SAH' [paper05]; paper06 'n of N (%) before every percentage' '10 of 75 patients (13.3%)' so small absolute counts (10/75, 6/90) explain wide CIs and the underpowered caveat [paper06]; paper07 'Favorable functional outcomes were achieved in 59 of 79 patients (74.7%)' [paper07]; paper08 dual n/N (%) throughout [paper08]; paper09 'Forty-three participants... with 51 stents... ISR was present in 14 stents and absent in 37' [paper09]; paper11 '18 patients (15.7%) showed a GIC' [paper11]; paper14 n/N(%) triad universal 'successful recanalization was achieved in 64 (86.5%) patients' [paper14]; paper19 denominators updated outcome-by-outcome (129 vs 126 for sICH; 88 vs 80) [paper19]; paper20 group-specific denominators repeated each time (222 vs 108) [paper20]; paper22 'no./total no. (%) when the denominator differs from randomized N' [paper22]; paper26 '111 of 161 patients [68.9%] versus 69 of 154 patients [44.8%]' [paper26]. (Applies to all genres; no genre exemption.)

10. EFFECT SIZE + 95% CI + (EXACT) P, MEASURE MATCHED TO OUTCOME TYPE (GENRE-GATED) — For association/regression/comparative papers, is every key estimate a NAMED measure (OR/aOR/HR/RR/common OR/generalized OR/beta) + 95% CI + P, with the measure correct for the data type (beta for continuous, OR/RR for binary, HR for time-to-event), crude AND adjusted shown for the primary outcome, and P reserved for the primary/confirmatory outcome while secondary/subgroup estimates carry CIs but no P? paper02 'adjusted OR=2.45, 95% CI 1.75 to 3.43, P<0.001' [paper02]; paper03 'occlusion length 5-10 cm vs <5 cm (HR 3.15, 95% CI 1.07 to 9.29)... residual stenosis >=30% (HR 3.08, 95% CI 1.79 to 5.30)' [paper03]; paper04 'BMI... independently associated with IIS (hazard ratio: 1.16 per kg/m2; 95% CI: 1.06-1.27)' [paper04]; paper05 'death (11.2% versus 1.5%; OR 8.04 [95% CI, 1.07-60.12]; P=0.042)' [paper05]; paper06 'an adjusted hazard ratio of 0.41 (95% CI, 0.14-1.25; P=0.12)' (point estimate + CI + P even when null) [paper06]; paper08 'aOR, 0.87; 95% CI, 0.43-1.47' (adjusted ORs rather than p-values) [paper08]; paper11 'ischemic core volume on baseline CTP (OR, 1.073; 95% CI, 1.036-1.111, p < 0.001)' [paper11]; paper12 'post-treatment DWI ASPECTS (OR, 2.401; 95%CI, 1.567-3.678; p < 0.001)' [paper12]; paper14 'OR, 1.11; 95% CI, 1.03-1.20; p = 0.007' [paper14]; paper19 'adjusted risk difference of 0.16 (95% CI: 0.04, 0.29)' alongside the relative RR; paper21 effect estimate with matching model + 95% CI per outcome [paper21]; paper24 effect carried by an adjusted common OR with 95% CI and p; paper25 P reserved for primary and per-protocol confirmatory only [paper25]; paper26 crude 'OR of 2.73 (95% CI, 1.73-4.34)' alongside adjusted 'OR 3.69 (95% CI, 2.04-6.68; P<0.001)', continuous outcome given a beta 'adjusted beta, 1.37 [95% CI, -0.31 to 3.06]' [paper26]. (DOES NOT APPLY to the pure procedural-comparative paper01, which by design carries NO OR/HR/RR and NO 95% CIs — significance is conveyed via exact P + absolute between-group numbers, e.g. '(14 vs. 8 min, P = 0.039)'; for that genre check instead that contrasts give comparator-named direction + two raw values + exact P; a reviewer note: absolute risk differences with CIs would strengthen its small-n non-inferiority safety claim. Also DOES NOT APPLY to a diagnostic/imaging-optimization study with no OR/HR machinery — do NOT manufacture OR/HR/RR with 95% CI; paper07/paper09 use detection-rate proportions + kappa + correlations + group mean+/-SD / the accuracy quintet instead. For non-inferiority disambiguate sidedness, paper23 'one-sided for the primary non-inferiority test vs two-sided for superiority.')

11. BETWEEN-GROUP DIFFERENCES STATED AS PAIRED VALUES WITH DIRECTION (X vs Y) + EXACT P, DIRECTION/CLINICAL MEANING BEFORE THE NUMBER — Is every contrast given as two raw values with a 'versus'/'vs' construction and exact P (never a lone P or lone group value), with a direction adjective ('elevated/higher/better/more favorable') placed before the statistic so meaning precedes magnitude? paper01 'Compared to TFA, the TRA cohort exhibited significant delays in... (generalized odds ratio, 1.37...)' [paper21]; paper26 direction word first '...remained significantly higher in the EVT group than in the SMT group (111 of 161 [68.9%] versus 69 of 154 [44.8%])' [paper26]. (Applies to all genres reporting two-group contrasts.)

12. EFFECT MAGNITUDE TRANSLATED INTO PATIENT-MEANINGFUL / BEDSIDE CURRENCY — ABSOLUTE UNITS, PER-CLINICAL-UNIT SCALING, ABSOLUTE RISK DIFFERENCE, NNT/NNH, NATURAL FREQUENCIES — Is magnitude expressed in clinically familiar absolute units or per-one-clinical-unit, and is a relative estimate accompanied by an absolute risk difference and/or re-expressed as NNT/NNH or natural frequencies? FAIL: only relative/abstract metrics with no decision-relevant absolute. paper01 magnitude in minutes/hours/days/0-10 pain 'median bed rest time (TBA: 6, TRA: 6, TFA: 24 h; P < 0.001) and hospital stay (TBA: 3, TRA: 3, TFA: 4.5 days; P = 0.024)' [paper01]; paper03 fold-language 'an increased risk of 3.08-fold for those with >30% residual stenosis' [paper03]; paper04 per-clinical-unit 'The adjusted HR for BMI in relation to IIS was 1.16 per kg/m2 (95% CI: 1.06-1.27; P < 0.01).' [paper04]; paper19 relative + absolute 'adjusted risk difference of 0.16 (95% CI: 0.04, 0.29)' [paper19]; paper20 signature reusable clinical-voice move — natural frequencies ('about 4.5 of every 10 patients') and NNT 'number needed to treat to avert 1 death at 1 year of 5.4' [paper20]; paper22 signature move — labels effect 'clinically meaningful' and converts a ~12-point difference into 'a number needed to treat of 8.2 (95% CI, 3.1 to 13.3)', benchmarked against established reperfusion therapies [paper22]; paper23 systematic back-translation — every OR re-expressed as an absolute risk difference for a defined 'average patient', an NNT-style count, and a per-hour time cost ('one additional patient... for every 57 patients treated') [paper23]; paper24 NNT for functional independence with 95% CI [paper24]. (Applies to all genres; for procedural-comparative paper01 absolute units are the ONLY effect-magnitude vehicle by design; for a diagnostic study the absolute analogue is the Youden cut-off + sensitivity/specificity (paper14); paper25 note: NNT not stated — the absolute risk difference for sICH is the clinical-decision currency provided instead, so an explicit absolute-difference translation can substitute for NNT.)

13. DIAGNOSTIC/DISCRIMINATION RESULTS ARE A COMPLETE, CUT-OFF-FIRST BUNDLE (AUC + 95% CI + cut-off + sensitivity/specificity +/- NPV), WITH INCREMENTAL VALUE SIGNIFICANCE-TESTED — For a prognostic/biomarker/diagnostic paper, is the continuous marker translated into a usable decision rule (the actionable cut-off stated BEFORE the AUC/sens/spec), reported NPV/specificity-led where rule-out is the use, and is any model gain tested (delta-AUC with P or DeLong)? paper02 'optimal cut-off of D-dimer... was 2.27 mg/L... sensitivity of 64.6% and specificity of 86.2%, a negative predictive value of 94.6%, and AUC of 0.774' [paper02]; paper04 'AUC of 0.72 (95% CI: 0.62-0.83)... identifying 24.5 kg/m2 as the optimal cutoff.
