# Title and abstract moves

Apply these decisions after selecting the target journal and study family.

## Name the clinical object {#ta-clinical-object}

- Decision: put the population plus endpoint, predictor, test, intervention, or comparison in the
  title. Add the design or parent-trial identity when it changes how the claim should be read.
- Evidence: recurrent across all three families in the 32-paper corpus.
- Failure mode: vague thematic titles, marketing verbs, or a design concealed behind a broad claim.
- Check: can a reader identify population, question, and evidence level from the title alone?

## Match journal architecture {#ta-journal-architecture}

- Decision: reproduce the destination journal's required labels, casing, word limit, key-points box,
  and trial-registration fields; do not impose a generic structured abstract.
- Evidence: journal-specific label sets were stable within the corpus and varied substantially between
  BMJ, JAMA, Lancet, AHA, and radiology journals.
- Failure mode: correct content placed in the wrong house structure.
- Check: compare against the current journal instructions before final formatting.

## State one primary question {#ta-objective}

- Decision: make the objective a single answerable question naming population, exposure/test/
  intervention, comparator when relevant, and endpoint.
- Evidence: all families; grammatical surface form varied by journal and was not a universal genre law.
- Failure mode: stacked verbs, multiple undeclared aims, or an objective broader than the Methods.

## Lead Results with the cohort or comparison {#ta-results-opener}

- Decision: establish the analyzed denominator or randomized groups before the headline estimate.
- Evidence: nearly invariant in the measured corpus.
- Failure mode: opening with an isolated percentage or a soft-news fraction without denominator.
- Check: every important rate should be recoverable as n/N where the source provides it.

## Operationalize endpoints {#ta-endpoints}

- Decision: define endpoint thresholds, scales, time points, and analysis population on first relevant
  mention within the abstract.
- Evidence: strongest in trials and neurovascular outcome studies, transferable across clinical work.
- Failure mode: `good outcome`, `successful treatment`, or `positive test` without operational meaning.

## Close at design strength {#ta-conclusion}

- Decision: restate direction and clinical object in plain language; include an observed benefit-harm
  trade-off and calibrate verbs to the design.
- Evidence: consistent across observational, imaging, and trial families.
- Failure mode: novelty claims, guideline recommendations from observational data, or generic calls for
  more research that omit the actual finding.
