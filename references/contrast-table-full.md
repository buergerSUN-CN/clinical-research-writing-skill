# Clinical vs Basic-Science — Full Contrast Table

Distilled from 21 exemplar papers — a Nanjing neurovascular/stroke + neuroimaging group (single-centre observational, imaging/diagnostic-accuracy) plus landmark multicentre RCTs (NEJM/Lancet/JAMA, basilar-artery-occlusion thrombectomy). Every exemplar is quoted verbatim, tagged with its source paperNN; three genres are distinguished throughout.

The detailed version of the contrast in SKILL.md. Each dimension: the basic-science tendency to AVOID and the clinical move to DO, grounded in the exemplars.

## Intro opening

**Basic-science (avoid):** AVOID opening on a molecule/pathway/mechanism, measurement-physics, or the detector/technology as the headline subject — and avoid framing disease as an abstract knowledge gap or an 'understudied phenomenon'. In an imaging/technique study the trap is opening on the physics (CEST/EMR/NEMR steady-state, saturation efficiency, detector hardware) or on signal overestimation for its own sake. The clinical exemplars systematically do the opposite: across paper01-04 the opening centre of gravity is never biology (sentence 1 is never a mechanism); paper07 'Opens on disease burden, not physics — first sentence is epidemiologic ... before any CEST physics appears' [paper07] with the EMR/NEMR mechanism block 'sandwiched between the disease opener and the prognosis aim' [paper07]; paper26 keeps recanalization/first-pass-effect content OUT of the opening, where it 'appears ONLY in the Discussion as an explanation for the observed clinical effect — never as the headline' [paper26]; paper09 keeps detector physics (photon-counting, septa) entirely in the main text, never the opener; paper19/paper21 carry 'No biology/mechanism framing' [paper21].

**Clinical (do):** Open at the level of the patient/disease and the bedside DECISION: lead on standard-of-care + its quantified limitation/complication burden, or define the entity by clinical scenario, then funnel to a real-world dilemma; demote any mechanism to a 'because of' clause. ## Framing of question / aim

**Basic-science (avoid):** AVOID framing the gap as 'the mechanism is unknown' / a biological hypothesis / a void, and avoid stating the aim as 'to investigate the mechanism/biological role of X', 'to characterize a signal/detector', or 'to prove a technique works' — with no named patient population and no decision at stake. Avoid a method-forward title (method, statistic, or outcome-metric in the title) and 'first-ever' novelty hype. The exemplars subordinate mechanism deliberately: paper04 keeps prothrombotic/'obesity-paradox' biology 'subordinate — cited to motivate the clinical question'; paper02 quarantines coagulation biology to one hedged paragraph; paper19's title 'names the off-guideline gap (a time window) rather than a biological hypothesis' with 'No method, statistic, or outcome metric' [paper19]; paper21's title is 'Patient-and-intervention framing, zero mechanism/device/statistic/result' [paper21]; paper07's whole signature is TWINNING the technical aim to a patient-outcome aim ('The mechanism aim is always yoked to a prognostic aim' [paper07]); paper08 'Promises clinical "Insights" (decision guidance), not a finding/mechanism/model' [paper08].

**Clinical (do):** Frame the gap as a clinical/evidence/practice scarcity or equipoise for a precisely-named patient subgroup, then state one hedged, decision-oriented aim (a head-to-head management choice, a prediction problem, a diagnostic/prognostic value, or the ROLE of a therapy); keep title/abstract-purpose/aim/conclusion in lockstep, with restrained 'to our knowledge' novelty. ## Results presentation (incl. diagnostic-performance)

**Basic-science (avoid):** AVOID leading with fold-changes/expression/dose-response/mechanistic readouts or a bare P-value; avoid percentages without denominators, model coefficients in place of clinical currency, pruning null rows, and treating non-significance as 'no effect'. For diagnostic studies, avoid reporting a bare signal/accuracy metric divorced from denominators and a decision threshold. The exemplars avoid all of these: paper01 'NO OR/HR/RR and NO 95% CIs anywhere ... significance is conveyed exclusively via exact P + absolute between-group numbers' in 'patient-meaningful absolute units' [paper01]; paper06 'Numbers reported in the currency clinicians use: head-to-head percentages with inline P values ... not model coefficients' [paper06]. NOTE BY GENRE: therapeutic RCTs have NO ROC/AUC/sensitivity-specificity by design — paper25 'no ROC/AUC/Youden cut-offs or diagnostic-accuracy-vs-reference-standard reporting' [paper25], paper26 'no ROC/AUC/Youden cutoffs, no kappa/ICC reader-agreement statistics' [paper26]; for those genres the analogous error is importing diagnostic-accuracy idiom where the treatment-effect idiom belongs. Conversely, the binary-radiologist-read diagnostic-accuracy genre (paper09) reports sensitivity/specificity/PPV/NPV/accuracy as raw n/N against a reference standard and does NOT manufacture a ROC/Youden/AUC for that read; in an imaging-technique study the prespecified primary endpoint is image quality / diagnostic confidence (Likert) + SNR/CNR, with diagnostic accuracy reported as a secondary read.

## Discussion landing

**Basic-science (avoid):** AVOID landing on a biological model/mechanism/pathway as the contribution, treating a null as a failure, or opening the Discussion with biology before patients. Where biology is invoked it must be strictly instrumental (plausibility), and ideally reframed as clinical natural history, not a molecular pathway. The exemplars make mechanism subordinate throughout: paper02 'Mechanism is CONTAINED + hedged to one paragraph as biological plausibility' ('may reflect', 'presumably because'); paper03 'pathophysiology ... appears only to explain why a clinically observed predictor matters'; paper06 'makes a non-significant result decision-relevant rather than treating "P>0.05" as a failure' with 'Mechanism kept as hedged hypothesis, never the endpoint'; paper07 'Mechanism invoked to explain a clinical observation, not for its own sake'; paper08 'Mechanism as hedged support (never the lead).'

**Clinical (do):** Open with a one-sentence restatement of design + population + headline patient outcome, benchmark against named prior series/RCTs, subordinate mechanism as hedged plausibility (often with an explicit ranking signpost), translate the effect into bedside currency (NNT, time-cost), and land on an explicit clinical-implications / management paragraph (surveillance, risk-stratification, selection, do-not-under-treat / do-not-exclude, benefit-risk weighing). ## Conclusion

**Basic-science (avoid):** AVOID closing on a biological insight / mechanism-novelty claim, a 'further mechanistic work is needed' tail, 'first-ever' hype, an over-reaching causal claim, blanket adoption ('X works and should be used'), or a 'replaces existing approach' overclaim; do not drop the harm/trade-off, restate the signal/mechanism, or introduce new content. The exemplars invert this: paper03's conclusion is a 'Tight, decision-centred close (no mechanism, no model-performance recap)'; paper02 mirrors the abstract with 'no hype, no imperative'; paper06's close is 'a do-not-under-treat directive, not a "further mechanistic work is needed" tail ... No novelty hype'; paper26 'defers any practice change to future trials rather than asserting a mechanistic discovery', keeping causal language hedged to association.

**Clinical (do):** Close on an actionable, hedged statement scoped to the exact eligible population: state the strongest data claim firmly with the benefit-harm trade-off intact, then a hedged decision-oriented offer (often a memorable plain statistic, a first-line choice, or a named-actor + modal), and a measured call for confirmatory trials; no new content.
