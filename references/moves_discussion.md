# Discussion, limitations, and conclusion moves

Interpret the study's evidence without enlarging it.

## Open with the principal findings {#discussion-principal-findings}

- Decision: restate the population, principal endpoint, direction, and decisive estimate or trade-off
  before reviewing literature.
- Evidence: invariant across the corpus.
- Failure mode: a generic topic sentence or a multi-point numbered flourish detached from the primary aim.

## Benchmark one comparison at a time {#discussion-benchmark}

- Decision: state what a relevant prior study found, then explain agreement or difference using design,
  population, endpoint, timing, treatment, or measurement.
- Evidence: all families; named trial comparisons dominated trial papers.
- Failure mode: a citation cluster followed by `consistent with previous studies`.

## Separate plausibility from proof {#discussion-mechanism}

- Decision: use mechanism to explain a finding, mark it as plausible, and return to what the data can
  establish. Introduce it only when the source manuscript provides the premise or the user explicitly
  requests a reasoned hypothesis.
- Evidence: observational and imaging papers used mechanisms as subordinate interpretation.
- Failure mode: presenting an unmeasured pathway as the demonstrated explanation.

## Translate only supported clinical meaning {#discussion-clinical-meaning}

- Decision: distinguish availability, prognostic information, decision impact, treatment selection,
  and improved outcome; claim only the level studied.
- Evidence: central cognition-layer distinction in the corpus.
- Failure mode: recommending altered treatment or monitoring solely because a marker was associated with
  outcome.
- Sparse-source rule: do not invent a `should not change practice` statement either; absence of evidence
  for action is not proof that the study evaluated or rejected that action.

## Preserve benefit-harm symmetry {#discussion-tradeoff}

- Decision: discuss safety or cost signals alongside benefit and keep primary/secondary status visible.
- Evidence: particularly important in procedure comparisons and trials.
- Failure mode: carrying only the favorable endpoint into implications and conclusion.

## Make limitations inference-specific {#discussion-limitations}

- Decision: for each material limitation, state the threatened inference: bias, confounding, precision,
  transportability, multiplicity, measurement, or missingness. Draft only limitations stated in the
  source unless the task explicitly authorizes design-based critique.
- Evidence: all families.
- Failure mode: a polished four-item template whose limitations could fit any paper.

## End at the calibrated claim {#discussion-conclusion}

- Decision: answer the objective, retain the design boundary, and state the next evidentiary step only
  when it follows from a real limitation.
- Evidence: conclusions across families tracked the design-specific claim gradient.
- Failure mode: `warrants immediate implementation` from single-center data or a vacuous `future studies
  are needed`.
- Source boundary: do not append external validation, prospective confirmation, or implementation
  research by convention when the supplied source does not state that next step.
