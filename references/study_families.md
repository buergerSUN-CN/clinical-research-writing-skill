# Study-family routing

Choose one primary family. Add a secondary family only when the manuscript genuinely combines
designs.

## Observational and prediction {#observational}

- Includes cohorts, prognostic-factor studies, prediction-model development/validation, and
  interventional cohorts without randomized allocation.
- Use association or prediction language; report adjustment strategy and event counts.
- Distinguish predictor identification from model performance and external validation.
- Route reporting to STROBE or TRIPOD as appropriate.

## Diagnostic and imaging {#diagnostic-imaging}

- Includes diagnostic accuracy, imaging biomarkers, technique comparison, reader agreement, and
  prognostic imaging studies.
- Name the index test, reference standard, readers, blinding, thresholds, and unit of analysis.
- Report discrimination, calibration, agreement, sensitivity/specificity, or image-quality measures
  only when they match the actual question.
- Route diagnostic-accuracy studies to STARD; route prognostic imaging studies to STROBE/TRIPOD.

## Procedural comparative {#procedural-comparative}

- Includes access-route, device, workflow, rescue-treatment, and technical-strategy comparisons.
- Keep technical success, workflow time, clinical outcome, and safety as distinct endpoint families.
- State allocation and confounding control before interpreting differences.
- Do not recommend a procedure from convenience or technical success alone.

## Trial {#trial}

- Includes randomized superiority, non-inferiority, equivalence, pragmatic, and sham-controlled trials.
- Anchor prose to allocation, prespecified endpoints, estimand, analysis population, effect estimate,
  uncertainty, and harms.
- State a non-inferiority result using the prespecified margin; a nonsignificant superiority test does
  not establish equivalence.
- Route reporting to CONSORT and any applicable extension.

## Trial secondary analysis {#trial-secondary}

- Keep the parent-trial identity visible but state that the present question is secondary or post hoc.
- Separate prespecified from exploratory analyses and preserve multiplicity limitations.
- Do not inherit the causal or practice-changing strength of the parent trial automatically.

## Legacy aliases {#legacy}

- Genre A: observational or procedural-comparative.
- Genre B: diagnostic-imaging.
- Genre C: trial or trial-secondary.

These aliases exist for compatibility with the original 32-paper build and `--genre A|B|C`.
