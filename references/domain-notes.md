# Domain Notes — Neurovascular/Stroke + Neuroimaging Reporting Conventions

Distilled from 21 exemplar papers — a Nanjing neurovascular/stroke + neuroimaging group (single-centre observational, imaging/diagnostic-accuracy) plus landmark multicentre RCTs (NEJM/Lancet/JAMA, basilar-artery-occlusion thrombectomy). Every exemplar is quoted verbatim, tagged with its source paperNN; three genres are distinguished throughout.

## DOMAIN NOTES — Neurovascular/Stroke + Neuroimaging Clinical-Writing Style (MERGED)

> CORPUS: 21 papers with full per-paper analysis, spanning acute ischemic stroke (AIS), basilar-artery occlusion (BAO), large-vessel/medium-vessel occlusion (LVO/MeVO), carotid/vertebral stenting, subarachnoid hemorrhage (SAH), chronic ICA/MCA occlusion, and neuroimaging (CTP, ASL, CEST/APT-MRI, PCD-CTA). Recurring authorship clusters: a Nanjing group (First Affiliated Hospital of Nanjing Medical University — Sheng Liu, Hai-Bin Shi, Shan-Shan Lu, Fei-Yun Wu, Xiao-Quan Xu) and a Xuanwu/Capital Medical group (Liqun Jiao, Yan Ma), plus multinational/Chinese trial collaboratives (CMOSS, DIRECT-MT, ATTENTION, ANGEL-ASPECT, ORIENTAL-MeVO, OPENS-2, OPTIMISTIC, IRIS). All THREE target genres are richly represented, plus the landmark-RCT genre and a PRISMA-IPD meta-analysis: (a) single-/multi-centre OBSERVATIONAL (cohort, registry, PSM comparative-effectiveness, prognostic/predictor, post-hoc RCT-as-cohort); (b) LANDMARK / confirmatory RCT and its secondary analyses; (c) IMAGING diagnostic-accuracy and prognostic imaging-marker. The journal spread is wide (NEJM, Lancet, JAMA Neurology, JAMA Network Open, Stroke/JAHA, Radiology, JNIS/BMJ, European Radiology, Academic Radiology, IJS, Translational Stroke Research), which is why journal-box conventions diverge sharply (§5) and must be checked before reuse.

> SCOPE & CORPUS HONESTY (the true N and the in/out roster — reconcile this before quoting a count): the distinct paperNN IDs actually analyzed and tagged throughout this spec are **paper01–paper09, paper11–paper14, and paper19–paper26 = 21 papers**, NOT 26. Earlier framings of "22 exemplars" and a "26 papers (paper01–paper26)" header are both wrong: there is no continuous paper01–paper26 corpus. IDs explicitly OUT, and why: **paper10** = the DIRECT-MT post-hoc malignant-MCA-infarction (mMCAi) predictor study (European Radiology, co-led by Changchun Jiang) — it WAS analyzed and is re-incorporated below where its content is load-bearing (it is the corpus's only Hosmer–Lemeshow-calibrated model, §2; a member of the Eur Radiol prognostic-imaging line, §6C; and a source of the enumerated-Discussion-opener voice marker, §7); **paper15, paper16, paper17, paper18** = present in the extracted/ directory but NEVER analyzed, contribute nothing to these generalizations, and must not be cited. Any "house style" claim here is therefore drawn from a deliberately bounded 21-paper sample (heavy on the Nanjing/Xuanwu author chains) and should be treated as a SHARED neurovascular/neuroimaging clinical-writing house style, provisional rather than exhaustively validated — not one lab's idiolect, but also not a universal law of the field.

1. NEUROVASCULAR / STROKE + NEUROIMAGING SCALES AND CUT-OFFS
====================================================================
Master convention (universal across all 21): every scale is introduced ONCE with full name + abbreviation + numeric range + direction-of-meaning, every numeric cut-off is attached to a plain-language clinical LABEL, and the scale is RE-glossed in each display-item footnote so every table/figure is self-contained. Numbers never appear without their bedside meaning ("NIHSS ≥6 = moderate-to-severe"; "mRS 0–2 = functional independence"; "eTICI 2b50–3 = successful reperfusion"). The cut-off TRAVELS WITH THE NUMBER — outcome row stubs carry the exact mRS definition so a reader never leaves the table.

**mRS (modified Rankin Scale, 0–6) — the universal patient-centred functional endpoint.**
- Range/direction gloss, verbatim variants: "Scores on the mRS of functional disability range from 0 (no disability) to 6 (death)" (paper20); "ranges from 0 to 6, with higher scores indicating more severe disability and 6 indicating death" (paper24); "scores range from 0 to 6, with lower scores indicating less disability" (paper25); "The mRS is a 7-level categorical scale, ranging from 0 (no symptoms) to 6 (death)" (paper26).
- Laddered dichotomy family with plain-language labels (all spelled inline): **mRS 0–1 = "excellent" / "freedom from disability"; 0–2 = "functional independence" / "favorable"; 0–3 = "favorable" / "good functional status" / "independent ambulation".** "Patients with a 90d-mRS score of 0–2 were considered to have favorable functional outcomes, while those with a 90d-mRS score > 2 were considered to have unfavorable functional outcomes" (paper07); dual dichotomy "excellent outcome: mRS 0–1, functional independence: mRS 0–2" (paper08); full ladder "(mRS 0–1 vs 2–6; mRS 0–2 [ie, functional independence] vs 3–6; mRS 0–3 vs 4–6)" (paper23); "Secondary outcomes included excellent (ie, mRS score, 0–1) or favorable (ie, mRS score, 0–3) functional outcomes at 90 days" (paper26).
- NON-DEFAULT cut-off CLINICALLY JUSTIFIED for BAO: "This threshold [mRS 0–3] was selected to reflect the high severity and poor natural history of BAO, in which complete independence (mRS, 0–2) is uncommon and is consistent with prior BAO studies" (paper19; recurs as primary in paper20).
- Used in BOTH directions: outcome AND eligibility gate. Outcome "mRS > 2 at 90 days" and exclusion "baseline modified Rankin Scale (mRS) score > 2" (paper14); pre-stroke mRS <2 inclusion (paper11, paper13); pre-stroke mRS 0–2 = baseline independence (paper22); prestroke mRS as adjustment covariate (paper19/20/21).
- Reading conventions: primary efficacy reading of mRS is the ordinal 90-day SHIFT via proportional-odds common odds ratio (paper23, paper24), with a **prespecified contingency to a binary primary if proportional-odds is violated** — "As prespecified, functional independence was therefore elevated to the primary outcome" (paper22, after Brant test P=0.02); paper21 fell to assumption-free Wilcoxon–Mann–Whitney generalized OR. **Death is an mRS LEVEL, not censored**: "These patients still contributed to the primary 1-year analysis, as the mRS score includes death as an outcome level" (paper20). 90 days / 3 months is the universal functional timepoint; ascertainment by a stroke/neurointervention nurse via clinic or telephone is a recurring convention (paper05, paper08).

**NIHSS (National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale, 0–42, higher = worse).**
- Gloss "scores range from 0 to 42, with higher scores indicating greater neurologic deficits" (paper21, paper20, paper22, paper25).
- Roles: (i) disease/eligibility gate ("baseline NIHSS ≥ 6" exclusion for "mild stroke" paper08; "a score of 6 to 30" entry paper21; ≥6 = "moderate-to-severe" paper22/25; OPENS-2 10–20 paper24; "<10 at admission" = mild BAO paper26); (ii) baseline severity, reported median (IQR) ("11 [IQR, 7–16]" paper07); (iii) timepoint-subscripted to map scale to decision point — NIHSSinitial/admission, NIHSS24h (paper07, paper12, paper13, paper14); (iv) the END (early neurological deterioration) metric: "END was defined by an increase of 4 or more points in the NIHSS score within the initial 24 h" (paper08); (v) continuous predictor per one-point increment ("OR, 1.11; 95% CI 1.03–1.20" paper14). Specialty caveat: NIHSS under-weights posterior circulation — "Future studies should validate the use of scales such as the pc-NIHSS in assessing the severity of acute BAO" (paper26); ASPECTS predictive capability lower than NIHSS because of its narrow interindividual range (paper02).

**mTICI / eTICI (modified/expanded Thrombolysis in Cerebral Infarction, 0–3) — TECHNICAL/procedural success, never a patient outcome.**
- Canonical threshold = mTICI 2b–3 ("Successful recanalization was defined as grade ≥2b" paper02; "mTICI 2b-3 [19]" paper12, citation-anchored to Zaidat 2013; "achieved mTICI ≥ 2b" paper08; reported as proportion with denominator "123/157 (78.3)" paper26). Gloss "0 (no reperfusion) to 3 (full reperfusion)" (paper20). TICI 3 used as the higher recanalization bar for chronic-occlusion recanalization (paper03). eTICI 7-point, success = 2b50 = "50 to 66% reperfusion" (paper21, paper22). paper25's primary endpoint **first-pass recanalization** = mTICI ≥2b after a single pass before any rescue.
- CROSS-GENRE RULE: technical reperfusion (mTICI/eTICI 2b–3) is reported as a PROCEDURAL/quality descriptor or eligibility item, NEVER as a primary EFFICACY endpoint — primary endpoints are always patient-centred (mRS shift) and safety is sICH. mTICI is genre-specific: ABSENT from non-thrombectomy/chronic-prevention work (paper04, paper06).

**ASPECTS / pc-ASPECTS (Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score, 0–10).**
- Anterior ASPECTS, lower = larger infarct: eligibility band "3 to 5 (range, 0 to 10, with lower values indicating larger infarction)" (paper21); gloss "higher scores indicating a lower infarct burden" (paper24). Reported median(IQR) paired with NIHSS as baseline severity + early-ischemia burden ("8 vs 9, P<0.001" paper02). DWI-ASPECTS operationalized fully (10 named regions, 1 pt/positive region, 20%-involvement rule, normal DWI=10) with a derived actionable cut-off "A post-treatment DWI ASPECTS ≥ 6 best predicts good outcomes" (paper12).
- **POSTERIOR-circulation pc-ASPECTS is the correct scale for basilar disease** (a teaching point): "PC-ASPECTS ... posterior-circulation-specific, not anterior ASPECTS, because the lesion is basilar" (paper20); 10-point, subgroup split ≤8 vs >8 (paper26). NOTE internal-consistency flag: paper26 expands pc-ASPECTS as both "Acute Stroke Prognosis Early CT Score" (running box) and "Alberta Stroke Program Early CT Score" (footnotes) — do not propagate.

**SAH (hemorrhagic) scale family — paper05 only (a genre tell; ischemic scales correctly ABSENT):**
- Hunt–Hess grade (clinical severity), dichotomized 1–3 vs 4–5 — "Hunt–Hess grade 4 to 5 (OR, 3.13 [95% CI, 2.11–4.64]; P<0.001)" (paper05); modified Fisher grade (radiographic blood burden) 1–2 vs 3–4; mRS >2 = unfavorable. DCI anchored to a named citation (Vergouwen 2010). paper05 notes NIHSS/ASPECTS/TICI are correctly absent because the genre is hemorrhagic.

**Stenosis / occlusion-severity thresholds (guideline-anchored, procedure-bound):**
- Stenting eligibility tied to cited guideline (ESVS 2023): "carotid artery stenting (symptomatic stenosis > 50%; asymptomatic stenosis > 70%) and vertebral artery stenting (symptomatic stenosis > 70%)" (paper01). Intracranial/carotid restenosis anchored to NASCET + WASID landmark-trial criteria; "ISR was defined as ≥50% luminal narrowing within the stent or within 5 mm proximal or distal to the stent edges" plus a stricter DSA gold-standard rule ("accompanied by an absolute luminal loss >20%") (paper09).
- The residual-stenosis "success" cut-off is genre-/procedure-specific and INCONSISTENT across uses, so keep it bound: stenting success = <30% residual (paper01); recanalization success = TICI 3 + ≤50% residual (paper03); ≥30% residual is itself a restenosis PREDICTOR (paper03). Restenosis defined with BOTH an angiographic % AND a Doppler velocity cut-off: ">70% stenosis or total reocclusion ... A threshold of peak systolic velocities of 300 cm/s ... was used to diagnose >70% stenosis" (paper03). Imaging predictor as ratio threshold: "ICA wall collapse ... vessel diameter being <50% that of the contralateral ICA at the same level" (paper03).

**Hemorrhage / sICH adjudication — always tied to a NAMED criterion set, never narrative; given equal billing with benefit:**
- Heidelberg Bleeding Classification is the dominant standard: "sICH was defined according to ... the Heidelberg Bleeding Classification" (paper02); subtype-resolved "HI1 (scattered petechiae, no mass effect), HI2 ..., PH1 (hematoma < 30% of infarct volume...), PH2 (hematoma ≥ 30% ..., with mass effect)" and composite "sICH ... PH, rPH, SAH, or IVH, accompanied by neurological deterioration" (paper08); "an increase in the NIHSS score of ≥4 points or an increase ... of ≥2 points with any intracranial hemorrhage on imaging" (paper21). Alternates used and named: modified SITS-MOST (paper22, paper25 — operational "type 2 parenchymal hematoma ... combined with a neurological deterioration of 4 or more NIHSS points"), ECASS III as sensitivity-analysis alternate (paper26). HT (hemorrhagic transformation) is the umbrella term; sICH the patient-relevant subset; sICH ~12% benchmarked as "similar to ... large trials and real-world practice" (paper02).

**Stroke etiology / subtype:** TOAST categories (LAA / cardioembolism / other / undetermined), used native or named, as covariate (paper02, paper19, paper26).

**Imaging-physiology / perfusion thresholds (stated as eligibility/criteria, never as bare mechanism):**
- CTP hemodynamic-insufficiency: "a mean transit time exceeding 4 seconds and relative cerebral blood flow <0.95 within the MCA territory" (paper06); MTT and rCBF as "hemodynamic insufficiency" markers reported per-sec/per-mmHg with laterality footnotes, MTT an independent Cox predictor (paper04). RAPID/iSchemaView core thresholds: ischemic core rCBF<30% (rCBF<20% for early presenters), hypoperfused Tmax>6s, mismatch = hypoperfused−core, HIR = Tmax>10s/Tmax>6s; eponymic "ghost infarct core" (GIC) = CTP overestimation of FIV by >10 mL (paper11); ADC<620×10⁻⁶ mm²/s core threshold, infarct-core 70–100 mL eligibility band (paper21). CEST/APT saturation-power optimum (1 μT) is the technical deliverable but consistently yoked to the mRS endpoint, not reported as a bare physics result (paper07).

**Other named scales / cut-offs:** BATMAN (Basilar Artery on CTA score, 10-pt, clot burden + collaterals, split ≤8 vs >8, paper26); AOL / mAOL (Arterial Occlusive Lesion, 0–3; patency = 2 or 3, paper22, paper24, paper25); Barthel Index (0–100; 95/100 = ADL independence; dead patients assigned worst score per SAP — paper20, paper22, paper24); EQ-5D-5L / EQ-5D VAS (QoL; death-equivalent and worse-than-death anchors spelled out: "0.00 is ... equivalent to death, with negative values being worse than death" paper22, paper20); aortic-arch Type I/II/III as access-difficulty covariate (paper01); stroke-workflow timing metrics ODT/onset-to-door, DPT/door-to-puncture, onset-to-CTP (itself an actionable predictor, cut-off ≤207 min paper11); BMI with Asian-specific cut-offs ("overweight (>23.0 kg/m²) and obese (>25.0 kg/m²) among Asians are lower than ... WHO criteria"; ROC cut-off 24.5, Se 100%/Sp 49% paper04); NRS pain 0–10 (paper01).

**Reader-agreement interpretive bands (pre-declared, vary by paper) — for IMAGE reading, not outcome adjudication:** weighted κ poor/fair/good/excellent at .40/.60/.80 (paper09); ICC 5-band slight→excellent at .200/.400/.600/.800 (paper12); Cohen's κ ">0.75 = excellent" (paper13); weighted κ 0.90 for ASPECTS in a therapeutic RCT (paper21); κ 0.842 for ASL reperfusion read (paper14); κ 0.67 (vs site) / 0.92 (inter-reviewer) for first-pass recanalization measurement quality (paper25).

2. REPORTING STANDARDS — AS ACTUALLY USED (the central, non-obvious finding)
====================================================================
The single most consistent finding across the corpus: **formal reporting-guideline acronyms are LARGELY ABSENT or used selectively; the journal's mandated structured scaffold does the standardization work — substance over checklist-citation is the norm.** The acronym, when present, is at most a single Methods sentence.

- **CONSORT** — the declared standard for several individual RCTs (paper22 "reported according to CONSORT ... guidelines"; paper25 "followed the ... CONSORT ... reporting guideline"), but NEVER literally named in NEJM paper21 despite full substantive compliance ("Fig 1 flow, ITT primary, prespecified outcomes, registration, harms reporting"); paper24 is CONSORT-compliant by construction without naming it. A **CONSORT-STYLE flow/patient-accounting apparatus appears in observational work too** — flow chart figure (paper05, 07, 08, 09, 13, 14) or prose cascade ("991→315" paper26; "4752 screened → matched" paper01; "1057 → ... 65 included" paper05; "347 ... 58 excluded ... 37 excluded ... Thus, 252" paper03; registry "From March 2017 ... consecutive patients" paper19). CONSORT-with-true-randomization is genre-correctly absent from all observational papers.
- **STROBE** — declared by full name in only a handful, and the declaration tracks the PHASE's design: paper09 (AHA/Stroke), paper20 ("reported in accordance with the ... STROBE ... reporting guideline" — for the OBSERVATIONAL extension of an RCT, not CONSORT), paper26 ("adhered to the STROBE ... statement"). Strikingly NOT named in many STROBE-eligible observational papers (paper01/02/03, paper05/06/08, paper11/12/13, paper19) where rigor is instead conveyed by journal house-structure + named external clinical standards.
- **STARD** — NEVER cited by name, even in the diagnostic/prognostic-accuracy papers (paper09, paper11, paper14) where it is the canonical guideline; those are "STARD-shaped in substance, STROBE-declared (or guideline-silent) in form." DOWNSTREAM SAFETY RULE: do NOT instruct a writer to "declare STARD" as observed corpus practice — STARD appears nowhere in these 21 papers; if STARD is raised at all it is a NORMATIVE recommendation the corpus does not actually follow, not a house convention to imitate.
- **TRIPOD** — NEVER appears, despite several papers deriving prediction tools (ROC cut-off paper02; regression-coefficient risk score paper03; multivariable logistic prediction models with ROC/AUC paper10/11/12/13). A genuine reporting gap: for most derived tools there is no calibration, no held-out/cross-validation, in-sample Youden cut-offs, and paper03 states its score "could not be verified in an independent dataset." The SOLE corpus exception is **paper10** (the DIRECT-MT post-hoc mMCAi predictor), which — because it is the one paper building a SINGLE combined prediction model — reports model **calibration via the Hosmer–Lemeshow test**; this is NOT a corpus-wide convention (the other combined/integrated-model papers, e.g. paper13/paper14, report AUC WITHOUT calibration), and TRIPOD itself is still never named even there. (Note: most multivariable models in the corpus are explanatory/prognostic-association with a force-retained exposure, NOT derived risk calculators, so TRIPOD non-declaration is often genre-appropriate.)
- **PRISMA-IPD + ICEMAN + PROSPERO** for the IPD meta-analysis (paper23): "reported in accordance with the PRISMA guidelines for individual patient data meta-analyses ... and was registered with PROSPERO (CRD42023411986)"; ICEMAN for subgroup-credibility; the surgical STROBE-equivalent **STROCSS** for a post-hoc-RCT-reported-as-cohort (paper04 — a deliberate genre signal that the secondary analysis is reported as a cohort, not the trial).

**What stands IN for a named guideline (the de-facto reporting standard):**
1. The journal's structured scaffold (Radiology Background/Purpose/Materials and Methods/Results/Conclusion + Summary/Key Results; JAMA PICO headers + Question/Findings/Meaning; NEJM IMRaD; European Radiology structured Declarations + Methodology bullet block + Key Points).
2. "Consecutive" patients + defined enrollment window + single-/multi-centre framing + numbered inclusion/exclusion + final-N statement (and flowchart).
3. Reader methodology: N readers with stated years of experience, blinding scope, senior adjudicator for discrepancies, an agreement statistic (κ/ICC) interpreted against a pre-stated band.
4. Trial-emulation/adjudication language importing RCT rigor into observational work: ITT assignment, named strategy arms (EVTpri/BMMpri, paper08), prespecified sensitivity/subgroup analyses, "independent outcome committee and ... imaging core laboratory, both blinded to treatment allocation" (paper06).
5. Pre-stated distribution-driven test selection + pre-specified multivariable entry rule + named software/version + two-sided α.

DOWNSTREAM RULE: decide whether to ADD explicit guideline declarations as an upgrade or PRESERVE the corpus's actual (often guideline-silent) convention — but if added, match the acronym to genre AND phase: STROBE for observational, CONSORT for RCT, PRISMA-IPD for synthesis. Do NOT add STARD (the corpus never uses it) and do NOT add TRIPOD as if observed (it appears nowhere, and only paper10 even reports calibration).

3. COHORT / REGISTRATION / ETHICS CONVENTIONS
====================================================================
**Cohort descriptor formula — "three-markers-in-one-breath" = consecutive enrollment + dated window + institution type:**
- "Between February 2018 and August 2021, consecutive patients with AIS who underwent thrombectomy at our single comprehensive stroke center were retrospectively enrolled" (paper02); "From June 2016 to August 2023, consecutive patients with symptomatic non-acute ICAO ..." (paper03); "From March 2017 to October 2021, we retrospectively reviewed consecutive patients with AIS" (paper14); "patients enrolled from 48 centers between March 2017 and April 2024" (paper19); "Between October 2, 2020, and May 18, 2022, a total of 1504 patients underwent screening at 46 centers" (paper21); "between April 30, 2021, and July 16, 2023" (paper25). Multicenter with database-provenance disclosure: "six comprehensive center databases ... five maintained retrospective databases, while one maintained a prospective database" (paper08); registry-grade "the ATTENTION registry—a nationwide, prospective, multicenter, pragmatic registry" (paper19). "Single comprehensive stroke center" / "our stroke center" is the standard self-description; "prospectively maintained database" recurs (paper03/04). "Real-World" in the title is a deliberate practice-relevance/credibility signal (paper26).
- Patient-flow accounting is exhaustive: exact exclusion counts + reasons, denominators that shift outcome-by-outcome and are restated, loss-to-follow-up with exact timing ("six patients (3.6%) were either lost to follow-up (n=4) or died from nonstroke causes (n=2) at 6, 6, 9, 12, 13, and 22 months" paper04), crossover accounting patient-by-patient ("Twelve patients (2.1%) crossed over ... 14 patients had protocol violations" paper22; "10 medical→surgical, 12 surgical→medical" paper04), follow-up completeness foregrounded as a quality signal ("All patients completed 90 days of follow-up" paper21; "330 patients (96.5% ...)" paper20). Pre/post-exclusion denominators disclosed rather than smoothed (paper23).

**Registration — present ONLY where a prospective trial/registry exists; absent (correctly) for retrospective observational designs:**
- Trial/registry number in canonical positions (abstract footer + Methods, often with completion status): NCT in the NEJM closing parenthetical "(Funded by ...; ANGEL-ASPECT ClinicalTrials.gov number, NCT04551664.)" (paper21); discrete labelled field "TRIAL REGISTRATION ClinicalTrials.gov Identifier: NCT04851457" (paper25); "registered with ClinicalTrials.gov, NCT04681651, and is now complete" stated thrice (paper24); Chinese Clinical Trial Registry "ChiCTR2000041117" (paper19); PROSPERO CRD + constituent-trial NCTs (paper23); "REGISTRATION: URL: https://www.clinicaltrials.gov; Unique identifier: NCT01758614" (paper06); post-hoc reuse of the parent NCT under IJS's "Research registration UIN" field (paper04). **NCT numbers double as forward-pointing devices** — citing ongoing/companion trials in Limitations/Future Directions (DUSK NCT05983757 paper22; ongoing RCTs NCT04167527/NCT03796468 paper08). Retrospective single-/multi-centre papers carry only an IRB number (paper05 "2023-SR-105"; paper08 "2021-NT-28"; paper26 "SYSKY-2024-602-01") — an explicit convention, not an oversight.

**Ethics / consent — three genre-conditioned patterns, all (mostly) China-based:**
- Retrospective → IRB approval + Helsinki + design-justified consent WAIVER + data-availability: "research protocol received approval from our institution's ethics committee. Informed consent was waived due to the retrospective design" (paper01, named IRB "The First Affiliated Hospital with Nanjing Medical University", review no. 2021-NT-28, given twice); "Written informed consent was waived due to the nature of a retrospective study" (paper12); "Informed consent from the patients was waived due to the retrospective design" (paper26). paper01 gives the ethics block TWICE (Methods + dedicated section).
- Prospective → WRITTEN informed consent OBTAINED: "written informed consent was obtained from each patient" (paper07); full prospective block + Helsinki + ICH-GCP (paper21).
- Secondary / extended-follow-up → ethics covers parent trial AND the secondary analysis SEPARATELY: "The CMOSS protocol was approved by the institutional review boards at all 13 participating sites ... the post hoc analysis ... received approval from the institutional review board of Xuanwu Hospital" (paper06); "Written informed consent was provided by all participants for the CMOSS trial and informed consent was waived for its retrospective design of the present study" (paper04); an unusually DETAILED telephone/verbal-consent justification for a geographically dispersed follow-up cohort (paper20).
- Surrogate/proxy consent is a domain norm (acute stroke = impaired patients): "patients or their surrogates" (paper25), "patients or legal representatives" (paper22), "patients or proxies" (paper23). HIPAA-non-applicability stated when conducted outside the US (paper19). Exclusions are differential-diagnosis-aware (trauma/moyamoya/dissection/AVM excluded by name, paper05) with a baseline-function floor.

**Independent-governance apparatus (the RCT credibility engine, enumerated):** steering committee, IRB, DSMB, independent CRO, blinded clinical-event adjudication committee (efficacy), independent AE committee (safety), central imaging core lab, independent statistician (paper22, paper24, paper25); named IPD collaboration + data-verification audit trail ("FC, KMT, MK, and DN accessed and verified the underlying data" paper23). **Operator/center credentialing** as a procedural-trial convention: "neurointerventionalists who met prespecified credentialing criteria (>5 years' experience and >80 previous thrombectomy procedures), at centers that performed more than 100 thrombectomy procedures per year" (paper22). Blinding-not-feasible candor where applicable (paper19).

**Front/back matter (standard clinical-journal furniture, present across the corpus):** named Funding with grant numbers tied to specific investigators (group signature: NSFC "82171907 to Shan-shan Lu; 81971613 to Hai-Bin Shi" paper11/12/13; Jiangsu "BE2022809 to SL" paper01/08; National Health Commission "2011BAI08B00" paper04/06); competing-interest statements (blunt "Disclosures: None." or itemized); industry-employment COI + editor recusal where applicable ("Yi-Cheng Hsu is an employee of Siemens. Shan-shan Lu is a guest editor ... did not participate in the selection or review processes" paper07; Siemens-employee co-author whose software is used, paper09/14); named scientific guarantor; data-availability statement; CRediT-style author contributions ("Guarantors of integrity of entire study ..."); funding-independence boilerplate "The funders ... had no role in study design, data collection ..." (paper24/25) and data-integrity attestation "The authors vouch for the accuracy and completeness of the data" (paper22); cohort-overlap disclosure with PMID + aim-difference (paper07); editorial-handling transparency (paper06). Author credentialing is clinical (degree suffixes on every author, equal-contribution footnotes, named collaborative group with Appendix member list).

4. EFFECT-SIZE & STATISTICAL REPORTING IDIOM (genre-differentiated)
====================================================================
SHARED across all genres — the **n/N(%) triad**: never a bare percentage; every event count carries numerator BEFORE percentage AND its group-specific denominator, repeated/updated per outcome so small counts and wide CIs stay transparent. "good functional status ... 35.7% (46 of 129) ... and 26.1% (23 of 88) of controls" (paper19); "99 of 222 patients (44.6%) ... and 21 of 108 (19.4%)" (paper20); "10.5%, 2/19" (paper01, even prior-literature rates carry their fraction). **Between-group contrasts written as paired values + exact P with the clinical adjective/direction BEFORE the numbers**, never a bare P ("less commonly had large-artery atherosclerosis stroke etiology (12.5% vs 32.3%, P=0.010)" paper02). Baseline balance quantified via SMD, NOT baseline P-values (paper08, paper26).

**Effect measure matched to data type (every estimate paired with 95% CI; direction/clinical meaning stated BEFORE the number):**
- Ordinal mRS shift → common OR / Wilcoxon–Mann–Whitney generalized OR: "adjusted common odds ratio, 2.55; 95% CI, 1.60-4.08" (paper20); "generalized odds ratio, 1.37; 95% confidence interval, 1.11 to 1.69; P = 0.004" (paper21).
- Dichotomized binary → OR/aOR or RR/rate ratio (registry pairs absolute risk difference): "EVTpri was not associated ... (adjusted odds ratio [aOR], 0.87; 95% confidence interval [CI], 0.43–1.47)" (paper08); "death (11.2% versus 1.5%; ... OR 8.04 [95% CI, 1.07–60.12]; P=0.042)" (paper05); "IPTW-adjusted risk ratio [RR], 1.67; P = .008" with "adjusted risk difference of 0.16 (95% CI: 0.04, 0.29)" (paper19); "adjusted rate ratio, 2.23; 95% CI, 1.51-3.29" (paper20).
- Time-to-event → Kaplan–Meier + Cox HR + log-rank: "adjusted hazard ratio, 0.62; 95% CI, 0.45-0.84" (paper20); "an adjusted hazard ratio of 0.41 (95% CI, 0.14–1.25; P=0.12)" (paper06); cumulative probability with per-timepoint CI "2y 13.3% (5.5%–21.2%)" (paper06); event rates at fixed timepoints 3/6/12/24/36 mo (paper03).
- Continuous (QoL, NIHSS, volume) → β coefficient / mean difference; imaging-prognostic → group mean±SD vs mean±SD + p + correlation r ("higher APT# ... 5.38 ± 0.67 vs. 4.77 ± 0.71, p = 0.001" and "r = −0.369, p = 0.01" paper07).
- Diagnostic/prognostic accuracy → ROC bundle: ROC → AUC(95% CI; P) → optimal/Youden cut-off → Se/Sp (and NPV) → THEN dichotomize the cohort. Cut-off stated BEFORE the metric ("Setting [marker] ≥ [cut-off] ... optimal performance (AUC ...; sensitivity ...; specificity ...)" paper10/11/12/13). D-dimer cut-off 2.27 mg/L (Se 64.6%, Sp 86.2%, NPV 94.6%, AUC 0.774) — NPV-led for rule-OUT (paper02); incremental ΔAUC significance-tested not eyeballed ("0.762 (sensitivity 70.3%, specificity 84.2%)" ... "significantly higher than ΔNWU alone (p = 0.045)" paper14); DeLong head-to-head AUC (paper12, paper13); single combined model with Hosmer–Lemeshow calibration (paper10, the sole calibrated model in the corpus); full sens/spec/PPV/NPV/accuracy quintet each paired with its raw fraction ("sensitivity of 100.0% (14/14) ... overall accuracy of 98.0% (50/51)" paper09); Bland-Altman/limits-of-agreement (paper11).

**Causal-inference & robustness by genre:**
- Observational treatment study: confounding-control engine = PSM (paper01 1:1:2 caliper 0.20; paper05 reports SMD 0.0034 + variance ratio 1.019; paper26 1:1 nearest-neighbor caliper 0.2) and/or IPTW + instrumental-variable analysis side-by-side + multiple imputation (MICE, Rubin's rules) + sensitivity analyses; method interpreted for the reader ("IPTW generates a pseudo-population with balanced baseline covariates, mimicking a randomized trial" paper19; "the stronger association observed after adjustment likely reflects the control of negative confounding" paper26). Multivariable model with EXPOSURE FORCE-RETAINED (paper05, paper08). Variable-selection rule stated quantitatively: univariate P<0.10 → multivariable (paper02/08), P<0.1 backward-stepwise Cox (paper03), P<0.15 entry/retain P<0.05 (paper04), p<0.1 (paper11) / p<0.05 (paper12/13). Collinearity handled transparently (VIF<10/tolerance>0.1 paper02; Model 1/Model 2 split because ASPECTS↔volume ρ=−0.847, paper12).
- RCT: ITT primary; prespecified interim analyses with O'Brien–Fleming alpha spending ("0.05 ... adjusted to ... 0.046" paper21, stopped early for efficacy); single prespecified primary carries the only P, secondary/subgroup are estimation-only ("the reported CIs should not be used for hypothesis testing" paper25; "Confidence intervals for secondary clinical outcomes were not adjusted for multiplicity" paper22); transparency about journal-requested OR→RR switch; honest method-switch-at-zero-events (report absolute risk difference when sICH=0, footnoted, paper25).
- Secondary RCT analysis: ITT primary + per-protocol concordance; prespecified subgroups; clinically standard covariate set (age, prestroke mRS, onset-to-randomization, NIHSS) (paper20).
- IPD meta-analysis (paper23): one-step mixed-effects (random intercept + random treatment slope); within-trial-effects-first to avoid ecological bias; non-inferiority margin back-translated to a clinical absolute ("0·82 ... analogous to 5% absolute difference in functional independence"); one-sided p for non-inferiority vs two-sided for superiority disambiguated in footnotes; null = "We did not establish non-inferiority" (failure-to-establish, never equivalence); confounding-by-indication named to dismiss observational comparators; an **"average patient" device** (age 71, NIHSS 16, ASPECTS 9) so an OR becomes an absolute probability/ARD.

**DECISION-CENTRED EFFECT TRANSLATION (a signature move):** re-express effects as **NNT (with its own CI)** and natural frequencies — "achieved in about 4.5 of every 10 patients ... yielding a number needed to treat of 4" / "number needed to treat to avert 1 death at 1 year of 5.4" (paper20); "a number needed to treat of 8.2 (95% CI, 3.1 to 13.3)" (paper22); per-hour time-cost ("every hour of delay ... approximately 6% less functional independence" paper23); benchmarked against established reperfusion therapy magnitudes (paper22, paper23). (Several observational/RCT papers do not compute NNT but leave absolute risk differences from which it is derivable.)

**Null & harm language (a strong recurring voice marker):** absence-of-evidence phrasing, not "no difference" — "There was no evidence of a difference in mortality" (paper19), "does not appear to be lower" (paper06); underpowered directional signal flagged "numerically more common" (paper21); **harm reported with the SAME rigor as benefit**, honest wide CIs on rare events shown not suppressed (mortality OR 8.04 [1.07–60.12] paper05; sICH aOR 3.06 [1.01–9.26] paper08; IPTW-adjusted RR 11.01 [1.37, 88.26] paper19; HR 6.93; 0.87–55.39 paper04). Distribution-aware descriptives stated prospectively (median(IQR) vs mean(SD)); SPSS + R/MedCalc named with versions; two-sided α=0.05.

CONSISTENTLY ABSENT (do NOT manufacture for the observational/RCT genres): ROC/AUC/Youden, sens/spec-vs-reference-standard, formal interaction-P for subgroups (subgroup claims rest on separate stratified models / separate log-rank tests — statistically weaker, worth noting), formal inter-rater kappa for OUTCOME/SUBTYPE adjudication (handled by blinded dual adjudication + consensus tie-break; kappa is used only for IMAGE-reading). The diagnostic-accuracy idiom (ROC/AUC/kappa, sens/spec vs reference) belongs ONLY to the imaging-accuracy genre — and note STARD is never the named guideline even there (§2).

Table titles = full descriptive sentences naming groups + analytic stage (before/after PSM) + timepoint; denominators reprinted in every column header.
- **Radiology (paper19):** structured Background/Purpose/Materials and Methods/Results/Conclusion + a Summary box + a Key Results box in plain patient-outcome language; paper-wide boxed Abbreviations glossary; ICMJE COI + CRediT.
- **European Radiology (paper07, paper10, paper11, paper12, paper13, paper14):** structured Objectives/Materials and methods/Results/Conclusions; **"Key Points" box** (often Question/Findings/Clinical relevance) — terse clinician-facing one-liners ordered at progressively higher altitude (prevalence → independent association → clinical-utility/substitution); a **"Methodology" self-classification bullet box** ("● Prospective ● Observational ● Performed at one institution") — CAUTION, self-labels can be wrong: paper11–paper14 all say "case-control study" while the design is a single-arm consecutive cohort with post-hoc outcome-defined groups — do NOT propagate "case-control" uncritically; a **Declarations block** (Guarantor / Conflict of interest / Statistics and biometry / Informed consent / Ethical approval / Funding) with distinctive "Statistics and biometry" candor ("No difficult statistical analysis was performed ... the first author ... performed all the statistical work" paper11). (paper10 is the DIRECT-MT post-hoc in this same Eur Radiol line, sharing the structured-Objective bare-infinitive opening and the Key Points / Methodology / Declarations apparatus.)

- **Academic Radiology (paper01):** house header "Rationale and Objectives" (NOT "Background"); NO Key Points / clinical-relevance box.

- **Translational Stroke Research (paper08):** NO Key Points / structured-abstract subheadings — single logically-structured paragraph; unadjusted AND adjusted ORs tabulated side-by-side with paired P; before/after-matching encoded in headers ("P value" before vs "SMD" after); footnotes restate the SMD ≤0.1 balance cut-off so it travels with the table.

**Cross-journal common display conventions:** every display item is SELF-CONTAINED and AUDIT-READY — own alphabetized abbreviation glossary (each table re-expanding terms), denominators in column headers, every scale re-glossed with range+direction at point of use inside legends/footnotes. Display-item economy: main text carries only the patient-and-decision core (~2–4 tables/figures: CONSORT/PRISMA flow, baseline table, mRS stacked bar, outcomes table with Primary→Secondary→Safety section headers, subgroup forest); everything statistically defensive (per-protocol, unadjusted, sensitivity, mechanism/biomarker, vital signs) is offloaded to Supplement ("Table S#"/"eTable"/"appendix pp"; paper26 Tables S1–S15). Tables hold the numbers; granular safety (HT subtypes, distal-embolization counts) is deliberately kept in narrative TEXT. Figure legends are interpretive/stand-alone (embed AUC, log-rank P, cut-off+Se/Sp, restate denominator, restate the headline estimate so the figure both shows and tells); ordinal mRS levels translated to bedside VIGNETTES inside the legend ("0, no symptoms; 1, no clinically significant disability; ...; 6, death" paper20/paper21); forest-plot axes labeled in plain direction-of-benefit language ("Medical Management Better" / "Endovascular Therapy Better") with a "P for interaction" column; representative-case legends are mini case-vignettes that ALWAYS close on the hard outcome (90-day/3-month mRS, or DSA "confirms"), with positive/negative cases PAIRED on identical modality sequences; a dedicated "Measure of effect" column maps the estimator per row (generalized OR for shift, HR for death, mean difference for volume, RR for binary) with the exact covariate adjustment set quoted identically across display items; footnotes pre-empt over-interpretation (multiplicity/underpowered-subgroup caveat), reconcile missingness with clinically plausible causes (death, illness) not bare "NA", and use "NA, not applicable" for arm-inapplicable procedural rows (control arm has no mTICI).

6. GENRE DIFFERENCES (observational vs landmark RCT vs imaging-accuracy)
====================================================================
KEY GENRE RULE: ROC/AUC/Youden/sens-spec + Cohen's kappa belong to the DIAGNOSTIC/PROGNOSTIC-ACCURACY genre; TREATMENT studies (observational or RCT) carry rigor via effect estimates (OR/RR/HR/risk difference), causal-inference or randomization machinery, ITT/per-protocol, and adjudicated consensus — importing weighted kappa ONLY for an imaging-classification variable, never for an outcome.

**(A) OBSERVATIONAL (the corpus's largest genre — cohort / PSM comparative-effectiveness / registry / prognostic-predictor / post-hoc-RCT-as-cohort):**
- Causal register strictly ASSOCIATION-not-causation: "EVT was associated with an improved 90-day functional outcome ... although the risk of symptomatic intracerebral hemorrhage was increased" (paper26); "EVTpri was not associated with functional independence" (paper08). Positioned as hypothesis-generating; defers definitive practice change to future RCTs (closing verb "validate").
- Procedural-comparison (paper01): diagnostic-accuracy toolkit genuinely absent by design; the "accuracy" analogue is operationally-defined TECHNICAL vs CLINICAL success; effect carried in patient-meaningful ABSOLUTE units (minutes, hours of bed rest, days of stay, 0–10 pain), no OR/HR/RR, no 95% CIs; PSM is the confounding engine; Table 1 doubles as flow + before/after balance.
- Biomarker/prognostic cohort (paper02): univariate (P<0.10) → multivariable logistic (aOR, CI) → ROC cut-off → dichotomized groups; borrows STARD-style metrics WITHOUT being a STARD study (and WITHOUT naming STARD); agreement by blinded dual adjudication + consensus tie-break, no numeric kappa.
- Predictor cohort with time-to-event (paper03): Cox (backward stepwise) + KM + log-rank + Fisher; regression-coefficient integer risk score; surrogate (restenosis) re-linked to the hard outcome (recurrent ipsilateral stroke 25.0% vs 1.5%, P<0.01).
- PSM comparative-effectiveness (paper05 single-centre SAH; paper08 multicenter EVT-vs-BMM; paper26 BAO): exposure = patient subtype or treatment strategy; balance quantified (SMD/variance ratio); aOR/RR + 95% CI; explicit trial-emulation apparatus (ITT, named arms, GEE for clustered data, prespecified subgroup/sensitivity reclassification) but explicitly "NOT an RCT"; actionable message can be an EMBRACED NULL or a guarded "no overall advantage except a named subgroup" (vertebrobasilar, paper08).
- Post-hoc RCT secondary analysis reported as cohort (paper04 STROCSS; paper06 CMOSS; paper10 DIRECT-MT; paper20 STROBE extension): self-identifies the genre relentlessly; inherits and verbatim-reuses the parent's design/eligibility/pre-defined composite primary outcome (a secondary-analysis economy); reporting guideline tracks the PHASE (STROBE/STROCSS, not CONSORT); paper04 fuses within-arm prognostic Cox + ROC cut-off with a treatment-effect-MODIFIER subgroup analysis, transparently reporting a REVERSED effect across BMI strata with a wide non-significant CI; paper10 builds a single combined mMCAi-prediction model with Hosmer–Lemeshow calibration (the only calibrated model in the corpus), straddling the post-hoc-cohort and prognostic-imaging genres.

**(B) LANDMARK / CONFIRMATORY RCT and IPD META-ANALYSIS (paper21 NEJM ANGEL-ASPECT; paper22 NEJM ORIENTAL-MeVO; paper24 Lancet OPENS-2; paper25 JAMA OPTIMISTIC; paper23 Lancet IRIS):**
- Apex clinical-trial genre; PROBE/randomized design named structurally; causal Conclusion templates permissible (randomized) but DOUBLE-hedged to journal restraint: "support the consideration of [therapy] in carefully selected patients" (paper22, "consideration" not "use"); actor-directed hedge "neurointerventionalists may consider this ... treatment" (paper25). Phase matters: paper25 is explicitly phase 2 → evidentiary-ceiling statement ("not powered to draw conclusions on ... 90-day functional outcomes") + "merit a future confirmatory phase 3 trial." Endpoint type differs: patient-functional (mRS shift) vs procedural composite (first-pass recanalization without sICH — a joint efficacy-without-harm composite as the bedside-decision framing, paper25).
- Credibility devices that REPLACE diagnostic conventions: adjudication committees, core-lab blinding, prespecification discipline, NNT translation. Cross-genre borrowing: paper21 imports weighted kappa for the ASPECTS read (an imaging-classification variable) into a therapeutic RCT, but has no ROC/AUC/Youden ("this is therapeutic, not a diagnostic-accuracy study"). Statistical-defensiveness lives in Methods + footnotes, leaving Results/Discussion prose confident and patient-centred.
- IPD meta-analysis (paper23): non-inferiority machinery vs superiority in the trials; PRISMA-IPD + ICEMAN + PROSPERO; the "average patient" device; pooling apparatus (one-step mixed-effects, Rubin's rules, within-trial-effects-first); null = failure-to-establish.

**(C) IMAGING DIAGNOSTIC-ACCURACY and PROGNOSTIC IMAGING-MARKER (paper09, paper10, paper11, paper12, paper13, paper14, paper07):**
- Diagnostic-accuracy (index test vs reference standard) — paper09 (UHR PCD-CTA vs DSA), paper11 (baseline CTP core vs follow-up DWI), paper14 (CT-NWU index vs ASL-reperfusion reference, κ=0.842): full sens/spec/PPV/NPV/accuracy quintet each with raw fraction; Cohen's/weighted kappa with a pre-stated band; Bland-Altman/LoA; named accuracy biases owned WITH DIRECTION ("work-up bias," "partial verification bias," "recall bias" paper09; "selection bias" with mechanism paper11). paper09's PRESPECIFIED PRIMARY endpoint is subjective image-quality / diagnostic-confidence (5-point Likert), sized via a paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test, with SNR/CNR/blooming secondary and a binary radiologist diagnostic read reported as raw sens/spec/PPV/NPV/accuracy AGAINST pre-specified thresholds WITHOUT AUC/ROC — i.e. it is NOT a headline AUC-style diagnostic-accuracy paper; paper11/14 DO use ROC/AUC + cut-off (GIC endpoint; NWU prediction).
- Prognostic / surrogate-substitution imaging-marker — paper10 (DIRECT-MT post-hoc, mMCAi predictors → single combined model with Hosmer–Lemeshow calibration), paper12 (DWI-ASPECTS as surrogate of infarction volume → 90-day mRS), paper13 (FVH → 3-month mRS): patient-centred mRS/mMCAi held as the endpoint; univariate → stepwise multivariable logistic → OR (CI, p) → ROC/AUC with Youden cut-off → DeLong head-to-head AUC (paper12/13; paper10's single combined model adds calibration); the "substitution" archetype explicit ("might be a potential surrogate of infarction volume" paper12).
- Imaging optimization + prognostic-association — paper07 (CEST-MRI, 1 μT optimum): engineered to read clinical via TWINNING — every technical optimum yoked to a patient-outcome question (90-day mRS/NIHSS); uses DWI as reference for ORDINAL lesion-visibility grading + kappa but explicitly "no formal sensitivity/specificity vs a reference standard."

7. THE RECURRING CLINICAL VOICE (across all 21 papers — the shared signature)
====================================================================
1. **DECISION-FIRST, MECHANISM-SUBORDINATED at every checkpoint.** Title, abstract aim, intro aim, discussion opener, and conclusion all lead and close with the patient population and a named clinical endpoint; mechanism (coagulation biology, obesity paradox, vasospasm, EMR/NEMR physics, device physics, perfusion physics) is quarantined to the middle, hedged, "instrumental not the lead." Titles exclude mechanism words (NEJM "Treatment X of Condition Y"; Lancet "intervention(+SoC) for [condition] in [setting] (ACRONYM)"; JAMA "Intervention 'on' outcome 'in' setting + acronym"); the decision noun ("Value"/"Treatment") sits in the front slot. The bare-infinitive structured Objective for the imaging line ("To investigate/assess/determine the prognostic/diagnostic value of [marker] for [clinical endpoint]"; paper07/09/10/11/12/13/14) is idiomatic clinical voice, NOT the basic-science "to investigate the mechanism of." The "More importantly" rhetorical-ranking move explicitly demotes a mechanistic inference below a management directive (paper06, paper07). Physics is immediately recast into a patient/decision consequence (paper09: "a missed or overestimated ISR, an unnecessary invasive DSA, delayed intervention"). Signature actionable triple recurs verbatim across box/Discussion/Conclusion (paper02: "limiting the number of thrombectomy attempts, determining the timing of procedure termination, and guiding the use of antithrombotic/anticoagulant agents"); the Discussion opens with a one-sentence restatement of design + population + headline patient outcome before any interpretation, sometimes as an enumerated lead ("Our study demonstrated three main findings", paper10/paper11).

2. **EFFECT-SIZE → BEDSIDE TRANSLATION as the signature move.** n/N(%) → paired % → relative effect + CI, then re-expressed as NNT (with CI), natural frequencies, per-hour time-cost, or absolute risk difference for a defined average patient; surrogates converted to what patients feel (restenosis → recurrent stroke paper03) with lay framing ("Nearly one in five patients," "4.00 times higher chance"); benchmarked against established reperfusion-therapy magnitudes.

3. **HONEST BENEFIT-PAIRED-WITH-HARM balancing** — the "but also" construction, safety operationalized as rigorously as efficacy: "led to a greater likelihood of functional independence ... but also to a higher risk of symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage" (paper22); "albeit with increased odds of symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage" (paper19); benefit-with-explicit-safety-caveat ledger repeated near-verbatim across abstract/discussion/conclusion (paper26 3-part: benefit up → harm up → reassurance on mortality). The connective backbone (However · Nevertheless · Conversely · Moreover · Furthermore · In contrast) stages benefit-vs-harm and claim-vs-caveat tension.

4. **DISCIPLINED, EVIDENCE-SCALED HEDGING; no hype.** Asymmetric: data claims firm ("was an independent predictor"), forward-looking clinical-value claims hedged (may / might / could / appears to / has the potential to / "may assist clinicians ... in the decision-making process"); hedging tracks the statistics, not rhetoric. Recommendations conditional ("should future prospective studies validate ..., [action] may help"). Conclusions sometimes explicitly warn against MISUSE of a predictor (paper11: "Patients cannot be excluded from MT solely based on baseline CTP-derived ischemic core volume"). NOVELTY = GENERALIZABILITY / POPULATION / literature-GAP, not first-discovery: "To the best of our knowledge" / "To our knowledge ... first ... completed" / "in a population different from that in previous trials" — never "first-ever" hype; subgroups demoted to "hypothesis generating" / "only descriptive."

5. **ENUMERATED LIMITATIONS naming the BIAS + its DIRECTION/consequence** (First/Second/Third/Finally), often with a single-centre-retrospective caution opener, a mitigation pivot ("Fortunately ..."; limitation-as-defensible-design; limitation-to-strength "However, our study used PSM ... All of these are strengths" paper05), and constructive pairing to an ongoing confirmatory trial with its NCT. Framed around GENERALIZABILITY-to-patients (which patients the result does/doesn't apply to), grounded in a concrete population feature — the recurring China-only / single-ethnicity / high-intracranial-atherosclerosis caveat: "we only enrolled Chinese patients, who experience a high prevalence of intracranial large artery atherosclerosis ... may not be generalizable to other countries" (paper20, paper21, paper03, paper04); imaging-prognostic cohorts additionally carry an MRI-tolerance selection bias because severe/symptomatic-HT patients cannot undergo follow-up MRI.

6. **NULL / EQUIVALENCE results reported as fully as positive ones** (all values + exact P / CI), supporting non-inferiority-style safety messaging (paper01) and pre-empting confounding (paper02 blood-sampling timing; paper04/19 wide CI shown not suppressed); honest negatives surfaced not buried (paper09 SNR/CNR going the "wrong" way + the single discordant case; paper11 null prognostic rows kept "all p > 0.05"; paper13 honest a-priori power calc admitting underpowering).

7. **BENCHMARKING TO REAL-WORLD / NAMED-TRIAL DATA for credibility and the field's acronym fluency.** Concordance/discordance connectors ("In line with a previous study," "Consistent with these studies, our research also demonstrated," "consistent with," "similar to those in the [NAMED] trial"); discrepancies explained in CLINICAL terms (race uniformity, modality mix, ASPECTS imbalance) not statistical ones; literature situated author-first with n + headline effect. Anchoring trial/registry/guideline names recur as the evidence backbone: ATTENTION, BAOCHE, BASICS, BEST, BASILAR, DAWN, DEFUSE 3, MR CLEAN LATE, SELECT/SELECT2, RESCUE-Japan LIMIT, HERMES, SAMMPRIS, COSS, CASSISS, CMOSS, DIRECT-MT, ANGEL-ACT, ESCAPE-NA1, WAKE-UP; guideline + practice-pattern + evidence-gap TRIAD ("guidelines recommend X → an international survey shows clinicians do Y → there is still no clear evidence Z" paper26).

8. **PATIENT-AS-AGENT, patient-centred noun phrasing throughout** ("participants," "consecutive patients," patients who "achieved"/"demonstrated" outcomes, "surveillance," "follow-up," "individualized treatment strategy," "bedside," "in clinical practice"), not engineering/biology nouns — this is what makes device-physics-heavy (paper09) and perfusion-physics-heavy (paper10/paper11) work read as CLINICAL research. A single repeated take-home carries to bedside near-verbatim across Abstract Interpretation/Conclusions, the Research-in-context "Implications" panel, and the closing sentence (the prioritisation triad "should not prohibit, delay, or be prioritised over endovascular treatment" paper23; "without raising safety concerns" paper24). Three-beat clinical funnel in the Introduction: disease-burden epidemiology (with approximation hedges "≈", "up to", "an estimated") → "However"/"Despite" pivot to an unmet need framed as a bedside trade-off / knowledge gap with treatment consequences → single explicit "Thus, we conducted ..." dual/head-to-head aim. Conclusion = compressed re-tell + forward clinical hook, no new content, no novelty hype. Restraint markers (directional trajectory verbs "sustained/enhanced/preserved"; "noteworthy"/"despite" without superlatives) over hype.

8. SOURCE / REUSE CAVEATS (cautions, NOT conventions to imitate)
====================================================================
- PDF artifacts preserved verbatim in profiles but to be silently corrected on reuse: soft-hyphens ("D-­dimer," "real-­world," "­EVTpri," "dan-­SAH"), fi-ligatures ("signiﬁcant," "beneﬁt," "Identiﬁer"), and OCR typos (paper05 "neurysmal," "intravenetricular"; paper13 "imcomplete," "imging").
- Genre-mislabels NOT to propagate: European Radiology Methodology box says "case-control" for paper11–paper14 though all are single-arm consecutive cohorts with post-hoc outcome-defined groups; paper02 labels an estimate "adjusted common OR (acOR)" though there is no ordinal outcome (likely carried from a sister paper).
- Internal inconsistencies flagged for accuracy, not style: paper01 (≥7 F vs >6 F; abstract pairwise P vs Table omnibus P; 4752 vs 4487 denominator; duplicated Figure 2 "(e)"); paper03 high/low-risk split (132/120) not reconciling with Table 1 score-band sums; paper12 denominator (62 vs 66 for good outcome) and Fig 4 panel-skip (d)→(f); paper26 pc-ASPECTS dual expansion.
- Stratum/arm specificity to keep explicit when paraphrasing: paper04's high-BMI risk statement pertains to the MEDICALLY-TREATED subset only.
- Corpus-count caveat (see SCOPE & CORPUS HONESTY in the header): the analyzed N is 21 (paper01–09, 11–14, 19–26). paper10 (DIRECT-MT mMCAi predictor) is analyzed and re-incorporated above; paper15–18 were NEVER analyzed and must not be cited; there is no continuous paper01–paper26 corpus, so "26 papers" / "22 exemplars" framings are both inaccurate.
- Borrow Lancet/NEJM exemplars' DECISION-RELEVANT footnote elements (endpoint definition, cut-off, named classification, adjustment intent) and their patient-facing effect translation + action-rule + direction-aware enumerated limitations, but NOT their defensive footnote density (full adjustment sets, p-sidedness, per-cell missingness).
