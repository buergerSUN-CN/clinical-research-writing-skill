# Domain Notes — Neurovascular/Stroke + Neuroimaging Reporting Conventions

Field-specific conventions and this group's recurring voice. Read when you need to get the clinical/imaging machinery (scales, reporting standards, statistics) right.

"SCOPE & CORPUS HONESTY: This v2 spec is derived from the 14 papers (paper01-paper14) that have full per-paper analysis JSONs. NOTE that the extracted/ directory also contains paper15-paper18, which were NOT analyzed and contribute nothing to these generalizations; any 'house style' claim here is therefore drawn from a deliberately truncated paper01-14 sample and should be treated as provisional rather than a settled, exhaustively validated style. paper01-09 span access-route comparison, D-dimer/BMI/restenosis prognosis, SAH and qualifying-event outcomes, mild-LVO thrombectomy, CEST imaging, and photon-counting CTA; paper10-14 are the European Radiology imaging/diagnostic-accuracy line (DIRECT-MT mMCAi predictors; ghost-infarct-core predictors on CTP; prognostic value of post-treatment DWI-ASPECTS; post-treatment FVH; and ASPECTS-based net water uptake for tissue-level reperfusion). The corpus is dominated by a recurring Nanjing-centered author group (corresponding authors Sheng Liu, Hai-Bin Shi, Fei-Yun Wu, Shan-Shan Lu, plus core members Zhen-Yu Jia, Lin-Bo Zhao, Yue-Zhou Cao, Guang-Dong Lu, Kai Qiu, Xiao-Quan Xu, Chun-Qiu Su) at the Department of Interventional Radiology / Department of Radiology, First Affiliated Hospital of Nanjing Medical University (Jiangsu Province Hospital). It is NOT a single homogeneous voice: corresponding-author chains differ (paper07 corresponds to Yi Zhang at Zhejiang University; paper03/04/06 are CMOSS post-hocs co-led by Liqun Jiao/Yan Ma at Xuanwu; paper10 is a DIRECT-MT post-hoc co-led by Changchun Jiang). Treat the conventions below as a SHARED neurovascular/neuroimaging clinical-writing house style rather than one lab's idiolect.\n\nRECURRING VOICE: The style is clinical-decision-, outcome-, and (for imaging) reference-standard-anchored, never method/algorithm- or mechanism-anchored. Titles are declarative and PICO-/design-forward: comparative ('X Versus Y'), prognostic ('Incidence and predictors of...', 'Predictors of...', '[Biomarker] as a predictor of [outcome]'), imaging-biomarker ('Prognostic value of [imaging marker] for [outcome] after [procedure]', 'X predicts/outperforms Y'), or diagnostic ('[technology] for Noninvasive Detection of [clinical entity]'). A study-design/parent-trial subtitle is FREQUENTLY appended (comparative/PSM and post-hoc-RCT papers: 'A Propensity Score-Matched Analysis', 'a post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial', 'results of DIRECT-MT trial', 'A Multicenter Observational Study') but is OPTIONAL — imaging/biomarker titles (paper07,09,11-14) and some predictor titles (paper02) omit it, and a case count can stand in (paper03, 'in 252 cases'). The group writes to each journal's exact structured-abstract schema: JAHA 'Methods and Results' + 'CLINICAL PERSPECTIVE' box; JNIS/Stroke/TSR Background-Methods-Results-Conclusions; Eur Radiol Objectives-Methods-Results-Conclusions-Key Points + 'Clinical relevance'. CAUTION on the structured boxes: the bulleted 'Key Points' box belongs to the Eur Radiol papers (paper07, paper10-14), and a 'Clinical relevance' statement is seen in paper07; paper09 is published in Stroke (AHA) and carries Key Words plus a graphic abstract but has NEITHER a Key Points box NOR a Clinical relevance box, so do not graft that apparatus onto a Stroke/AHA diagnostic paper. For Eur Radiol and similar imaging journals the structured Objective canonically opens with a BARE INFINITIVE ('To investigate/assess/explore/determine/evaluate the (prognostic/diagnostic) value of [marker] for [clinical endpoint]'; paper07,09,10-14) — idiomatic clinical voice for associations/predictors/tests, NOT the basic-science 'to investigate the mechanism of'. The group works in tight thematic research lines (net-water-uptake/ASPECTS imaging biomarkers; D-dimer/BMI prognostic markers; CMOSS post-hoc analyses; restenosis/poststent surveillance; CTP pitfalls) and mines flagship trials (DIRECT-MT, CMOSS) for secondary papers.\n\nOPTIONAL RHETORICAL FLOURISHES (minority, not defining): an occasional 'More importantly' (paper06, paper07) elevates the patient-care message above the biological one; an occasional 'Fortunately, we set a solid reference level...' (paper02) counterbalances a limitation with a reassuring design strength; predictor papers sometimes open the Discussion with an enumerated 'Our study demonstrated three main findings' (paper10, paper11). Conclusions are deliberately measured, sometimes explicitly warning against MISUSE of a predictor (paper11: 'Patients cannot be excluded from MT solely based on baseline CTP-derived ischemic core volume'; paper05: outcomes driven by severity markers, 'no relation with the pathogenesis'). NOVELTY is restrained to a single 'To our knowledge, this is the first...'.\n\nNEUROVASCULAR / STROKE REPORTING CONVENTIONS used throughout: (1) Functional outcome = modified Rankin Scale (mRS) at 90 days/3 months, dichotomized as functional independence (mRS 0-2), excellent outcome (mRS 0-1), or unfavorable/poor (mRS >2); report dichotomized or shift with OR/CI. (2) Stroke severity = NIHSS (admission and 24 h); mild stroke commonly NIHSS <6. (3) Early ischemic change / core = ASPECTS and its variants (CBV-ASPECTS, NWU-ASPECTS, post-treatment DWI-ASPECTS). (4) Reperfusion/recanalization graded by TICI/mTICI/eTICI (mTICI 2b-3 = successful recanalization; TICI 3 = complete); tissue-level reperfusion assessed separately by arterial spin labeling (ASL) — distinguish RECANALIZATION (vessel) from REPERFUSION (tissue), the 'futile recanalization' / 'no-reflow' concept (paper14). (5) Hemorrhage: sICH / HT defined by the Heidelberg Bleeding Classification, blindly adjudicated by neuroradiologists with consensus arbitration. (6) Stenosis anchored to NASCET (carotid) and WASID (intracranial) with clinically meaningful thresholds (symptomatic >50%/>70%; ISR = ≥50% in-stent). (7) SAH severity graded by Hunt-Hess and modified Fisher; complications include rehemorrhage, delayed cerebral ischemia (DCI), hydrocephalus. (8) Time windows stated in hours (≤24 h last-known-normal; 6-24 h late window); GIC/core thresholds are time-dependent (stratify rCBF cut-off by onset time, paper11). (9) NEUROIMAGING DIAGNOSTIC/PROGNOSTIC CONVENTIONS (the v2 focus): every imaging study names an INDEX TEST and a REFERENCE STANDARD — DSA for vascular patency/ISR (paper09), follow-up DWI/final infarct volume for true core (paper11/FIV), ASL for tissue-level reperfusion (paper14), infarction volume as the comparator metric (paper12). The two imaging genres diverge and must not be conflated: (a) DIAGNOSTIC-ACCURACY (paper09) reports sensitivity/specificity/PPV/NPV/accuracy as RAW numerators/denominators against a reference standard with NO ROC/Youden/AUC for its binary radiologist read, uses a paired SAME-PATIENT design against a surrogate comparator (simulated EID-CTA reconstructed from the same raw data, read FIRST with a 4-week washout to minimize recall bias, randomized case order) driving paired Wilcoxon/paired-t/McNemar testing, often LEADS with quantitative image-quality / diagnostic-confidence (5-point Likert) and SNR/CNR/blooming endpoints (its primary endpoint and sample-size basis were the subjective image-quality scores, sized via a paired Wilcoxon signed-rank test), reports radiation dose (CTDIvol, DLP) and a separate safety statement ('All participants successfully underwent PCD-CTA without adverse events'), and declares STROBE (NOT STARD — STARD appears nowhere in the corpus and must not be asserted as observed practice; if STARD is mentioned at all it is a normative recommendation the corpus does not follow); (b) PROGNOSTIC-IMAGING (paper10-14) reports discrimination as ROC with Youden-index cut-off and AUC (95% CI) plus sensitivity/specificity, ranks two markers/models with the NAMED DeLong test in paper12 and paper13 (paper14 instead compares two nested-model AUCs, p=0.045, WITHOUT naming DeLong), and reports prediction-model calibration via Hosmer-Lemeshow in ONLY paper10 (the RCT post-hoc that builds a single combined model) — paper13 and paper14 build combined/integrated models and report AUC WITHOUT calibration, so Hosmer-Lemeshow is not a corpus-wide convention. A DEFINING statistical signature of the prognostic-imaging line is COLLINEARITY MANAGEMENT: because correlated imaging markers cannot share one model, these papers compute the variance inflation factor (VIF; threshold >10, or graded 1-5-10) and then either SPLIT correlated markers into parallel multivariable models (paper12: rho=-0.847 between DWI-ASPECTS and infarction volume -> two models) or switch to STEPWISE selection (paper11: VIF 11.718/10.210 for hypoperfused-area/mismatch -> stepwise; paper14: VIF 2.583/1.057/1.116, moderate collinearity). Surrogacy between two imaging measures is argued with a Spearman/Pearson correlation coefficient reported against an explicit slight/fair/moderate/good/excellent band (paper12: 0.000-0.200 ... 0.801-1.000; paper11: Pearson r of overestimation vs onset-time r=-0.597 and vs core volume r=0.806), and method-comparison against the reference is visualized with a Bland-Altman mean difference + limits of agreement (paper11: mean -21.1 mL, LoA -124.7 to 86 mL). For this group the SUBGROUP/THRESHOLD analysis is frequently the clinical message itself: re-deriving the rCBF core threshold stratified by onset-to-CTP time and concluding it 'should be stratified by stroke onset time' (paper11), or stratifying a successful-recanalization subgroup (n=64) to expose 'futile recanalization' (23.4% poor reperfusion, paper14). INTER-READER reproducibility is ALWAYS reported (weighted Cohen kappa or ICC with 95% CI, interpreted poor/fair/good/excellent) from blinded, stated-experience radiologists with consensus/third-reader adjudication; the reference-standard strategy is justified to control work-up/partial-verification bias (2-tier reference; DSA only when clinically indicated 'to avoid unnecessary invasive procedures'). Eur Radiol papers (paper07, paper10-14) carry a designated scientific Guarantor and Methodology/Statistics-and-biometry declarations, and the Methodology line SELF-LABELS the design with bullets — '• retrospective • case-control study • performed at one institution' (paper11-14) or '● Prospective ● Observational' (paper07) — a label that coexists with, and should be reconciled against, the fact that paper11-14 actually enroll consecutive single-center retrospective patients (i.e., 'case-control study' is the journal's classification term, not a contradiction of the consecutive-enrollment framing). (10) STUDY-DESIGN NORMS: single-center retrospective analysis of a prospectively maintained registry of CONSECUTIVE patients (present in paper02/03/09/11/12/13/14, NOT universal — post-hoc RCT (paper04/06/10) and multicenter observational (paper08) papers describe the parent/multicenter cohort instead and need not say 'consecutive'); or post-hoc analysis of a named RCT (COSS, CMOSS, DIRECT-MT, SAMMPRIS, CASSISS); propensity-score matching (logistic-regression PS, caliper, 1:1/1:2/1:4) with balance diagnostics (SMD, variance ratio) for matched comparisons; univariate screen at P<0.10 (sometimes <0.05) feeding multivariable logistic or Cox regression (or stepwise when collinear); Kaplan-Meier/log-rank for time-to-event; reporting-guideline adherence declared (STROBE/STROCSS; CONSORT-style flow charts); IRB approval number, Declaration of Helsinki/GCP, and informed consent or waiver stated first in Methods; outcome ascertainment via independent outcome committee / imaging core lab (trial analyses) or a neurointerventional/stroke nurse specialist by clinic/telephone (single-center cohorts); blinded adjudicated reads WHEN the endpoint is subjective (sICH, restenosis, FVH/GIC presence, ASPECTS, perfusion maps, image quality) but appropriately absent for objective procedural-metric (paper01) or administrative-mortality (paper05) endpoints; an a priori power/sample-size calculation where feasible (paper09 on the image-quality primary endpoint, paper13). POPULATION CAVEAT recurrently noted: cohorts are predominantly Chinese/Asian, so derived thresholds (BMI cut-off given lower Asian overweight/obesity cutoffs; rCBF/ΔNWU/ASPECTS cut-offs) may need re-derivation in other populations; imaging-prognostic cohorts additionally carry an MRI-tolerance selection bias because severe/symptomatic-HT patients cannot undergo follow-up MRI. Same-field big-lab comparators worth emulating for trial-report discipline: Miao (ANGEL-ASPECT), Jiao (CASSISS), Jian-Min Liu (PARAT/DIRECT-MT), and international landmarks (SELECT2, RESCUE-Japan LIMIT, ATTENTION, BAOCHE, MR CLEAN-NO IV, HERMES) — all report the primary endpoint as a (common/adjusted) OR with 95% CI, co-report safety transparently, position the question against prior (often negative) trials, and write restrained single-claim conclusions, especially for null results."
