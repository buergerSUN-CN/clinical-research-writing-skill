# Section-by-Section Clinical Writing Playbook

Distilled from 21 exemplar papers — a Nanjing neurovascular/stroke + neuroimaging group (single-centre observational, imaging/diagnostic-accuracy) plus landmark multicentre RCTs (NEJM/Lancet/JAMA, basilar-artery-occlusion thrombectomy). Every exemplar is quoted verbatim, tagged with its source paperNN; three genres are distinguished throughout.

Use when drafting/rewriting a section: do the *clinical moves*, avoid the *pitfalls*, model the *exemplars*. `variant notes` show how moves differ across genres (single-centre observational / landmark RCT / imaging-accuracy).

## Title

**Goal:** Position the paper by its clinical payload in the title itself — the management decision/comparison, the precisely named decision population, and the patient-relevant outcome — with the study design/provenance appended as an evidence-grade signal, so a clinician instantly grades the level of evidence and sees the actionable deliverable. Never lead with a mechanism, pathway, or biomarker pathway.

**Clinical moves:**
- Lead with the clinical deliverable or decision verb (a comparison, an incidence+predictors, a diagnostic/predictive/prognostic role, or a head-to-head 'Intervention X versus Management Y'), not a biological construct or method/statistic word.
- Pin the exact decision population and indication with the bedside descriptor that makes the sub-question unresolved (e.g. 'large-bore neuro stenting', 'non-acute internal carotid artery occlusion', 'Acute Mild Basilar Artery Occlusion', 'Large Infarct', 'beyond 24 Hours', 'with Mild Symptoms').
- Embed the outcome in prognostically loaded, patient-affecting terms ('symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage', 'recurrent stroke', 'In-Stent Restenosis') and keep the patient-benefit adjective where it encodes value ('Noninvasive Detection').
- Append the design/credibility signal as a colon-suffix or subtitle ('Insights From a Propensity-Matched Study', 'a post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial', 'A Multicenter Real-World Study', 'A Randomized Clinical Trial', a sample count '...in 252 cases'); for named-trial secondary analyses put the trial acronym in the title to inherit its pedigree.
- Hedge novelty inside the noun phrase ('novel predictor', 'Potential of ... as a screening indicator') rather than hyping; keep no effect metric (OR, AUC, %) in the title itself.
- When a head-to-head substitution/comparison is the point, append it explicitly ('comparison with infarction volume').

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Leading with a mechanism, pathway, biomarker/receptor, or imaging-physics/algorithm variable as the grammatical subject instead of the management question.
- Naming the statistical machinery or model ('predictive model', 'ROC analysis') or a result/effect direction instead of the clinical role and decision.
- Using surrogate-only or radiographic-only outcome wording, or naming the whole disease without pinning the unresolved decision-relevant stratum, so the gap is invisible.
- Hiding the design (single-centre/retrospective/post-hoc/observational) when disclosing it would let readers grade evidence; or over-claiming originality ('first-ever', 'breakthrough') instead of a measured 'novel'/'potential'.
- Loading the title with effect metrics or omitting the population so the reader cannot place the work in a care pathway.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**
- Comparing Transbrachial and Transradial as Alternatives to Transfemoral Access for Large-Bore Neuro Stenting: Insights From a Propensity-Matched Study [paper01]
- Potential of BMI as a screening indicator for extracranial–intracranial bypass surgery in patients with symptomatic artery occlusion: a post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial [paper04]
- Ultra-High-Resolution Photon-Counting CT Angiography for Noninvasive Detection of Neurovascular In-Stent Restenosis [paper09]
- Endovascular Therapy versus Medical Management for Basilar Artery Occlusion Presenting beyond 24 Hours [paper19]
- Effectiveness of Endovascular Treatment for Acute Mild Basilar Artery Occlusion Stroke: A Multicenter Real-World Study [paper26]

**Variant notes:** Single-centre observational (paper01/02/03/05/14/26): title carries a sample count or design-credibility colon-suffix ('in 252 cases', 'Insights From a Propensity-Matched Study', 'A Multicenter Real-World Study') and a decision/comparison/incidence-and-predictors frame; imaging/prognostic single-centre titles (paper09/11/12/13) lead with 'Noninvasive Detection'/'Predictors of'/'Prognostic value of' + the eponymic clinical phenomenon (ghost infarct core, FVH) + the EVT/MT decision point, paper12 appending the head-to-head comparator after a colon. Landmark/secondary RCT (paper04/06/20/21/22/24/25): names parent-trial provenance honestly in the subtitle when post-hoc ('a post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial', 'A Secondary Analysis of a Randomized Clinical Trial'); primary RCTs use NEJM 'Trial of [Intervention] for [Disease+phenotype]' (paper21), Lancet 'intervention(+SoC) for [condition] in [setting] (ACRONYM): [stacked design tags]' (paper24), or JAMA 'Intervention on [outcome] in [setting]: [acronym/design]' (paper25); the bare 'Treatment of Condition' noun phrase (paper22) is the most minimal RCT form. Imaging/diagnostic-accuracy: the diagnostic-accuracy imaging title foregrounds the patient-benefit adjective on the clinical action ('Noninvasive ... Detection', paper09) — this is the diagnostic-accuracy GENRE, but note paper09 itself declares STROBE, not STARD, so do not infer a STARD provenance from the title register; paper07 keeps the technical parameter as grammatical subject but anchors the population at the end ('in patients with acute ischemic stroke') and relies on the journal MR section header for clinical identity; the diagnostic register otherwise surfaces as a point-of-care biomarker framed as 'predictor'/'screening indicator' (paper02/04).

## Abstract

**Goal:** Pre-summarize the inferential core in the journal's mandated structured schema, framing the gap as clinical equipoise/unmet decision and the aim as a decision/prediction problem; report patient-centred endpoints as n/N(%) FIRST then the matching effect estimate (OR/HR/aOR/RR) with 95% CI (+ exact P for the primary), give safety co-equal billing, and land on a population-bounded, trade-off-foregrounded, calibrated-hedge conclusion. Headline numbers must match Results verbatim.

**Clinical moves:**
- Open Background/Importance on disease burden + complication severity or the standard-of-care limitation for a specific subpopulation (concede SoC works, then pivot with 'however'/'but'), naming the precise evidence gap ('remains unclear', 'has not been well delineated'); state the aim with a decision/prediction/comparison verb ('evaluate the safety and effectiveness', 'explore the ability ... to predict', 'aimed to compare outcomes'), not a mechanism-elucidation hypothesis.
- Lead Methods with patient count + clinical anchors, label the cohort ('consecutive'/'retrospectively enrolled'/design pedigree), gloss every scale inline (range + direction-of-benefit), and define the patient-centred endpoint with its actionable cut-off BEFORE the statistics ('Good outcome was defined as a 3-month mRS 0–2').
- Report Results as head-to-head clinical contrasts: paired absolute values / n/N(%) FIRST (group-specific denominators), then the effect estimate template '% versus %; OR/aOR/HR/RR [95% CI]; P=' with explicit direction; for accuracy claims lead cut-off-first then sensitivity/specificity/NPV (rule-out) and AUC with CI.
- Carry every event as count(denominator, %); make abstract numbers match Results verbatim (same exact P values); pair every efficacy claim with its safety counterweight (e.g. sICH) and phrase nulls as 'no evidence of a difference', embracing an actionable null rather than treating P>0.05 as failure.
- Land the Conclusion on a treatment-selection / independent-predictor / screening-role statement, population-bounded ('In this cohort of patients with ...') and foregrounding the harm trade-off in an 'although'/'but had more...' clause, with calibrated modal hedging ('may favor', 'may add', 'appear to derive greater benefit') plus a confirmation-needed coda where design warrants; add registration (URL+NCT) and funding in the canonical parenthetical/field for trials.
- Where the journal mandates a value box, distil it cleanly: JAMA Key Points (Question/Findings/Meaning), European Radiology Key Points (Question/Findings/Clinical relevance), or AHA Key Points/Lancet 'Research in context' — each restating a finding as a stand-alone patient-value one-liner at progressively higher altitude.

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Reporting bare P values without paired absolute group values / numerator-denominator, or relative effects (OR/CI) without the absolute per-arm rates a clinician needs.
- Framing Background around pathophysiology/biology instead of clinical equipoise/unmet decision; stating a mechanism in the conclusion instead of the decision-relevant claim.
- Leading Results with surrogate/imaging endpoints, a coefficient, or a metric instead of the patient-centred functional outcome/proportion; reporting accuracy metric-first instead of cut-off-first.
- Reporting efficacy while hiding or down-weighting the safety signal; writing 'no difference' instead of 'no evidence of a difference'.
- Letting abstract numbers drift from the Results body; over-stating the conclusion as a treatment mandate ('should be used') or over-generalizing beyond the exact cohort/subgroup instead of a hedged, design-proportioned, population-bounded recommendation.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**
- Data from the CMOSS trial indicate that patients with BMI ≥ 24.5 kg/m2 are at a higher risk of IIS when treated medically only and appear to derive greater beneﬁt from bypass surgery compared to those with lower BMIs. Given the small sample size and the inherent limitations of retrospective analyses, further large-scale, prospective studies are necessary to conﬁrm these ﬁndings. [paper04]
- UHR PCD-CTA achieved excellent diagnostic performance for ISR detection, with a sensitivity of 100.0%, specificity of 97.3%, and overall accuracy of 98.0%. [paper09]
- There was no evidence of a difference in mortality at 90 days between EVT and control groups (48.1% [62 of 129 patients] vs 54.6% [48 of 88 patients]; IPTW-adjusted RR, 0.80; P = .10). [paper19]
- Among patients with acute ischemic stroke due to medium-vessel occlusion and moderate-to-severe deficits, thrombectomy led to a greater likelihood of functional independence than medical management alone but also to a higher risk of symptomatic intracranial hemorrhage. [paper22]
- In this cohort of patients with acute mild BAO, EVT was associated with an improved 90-day functional outcome compared with standard medical treatment alone, although the risk of symptomatic intracerebral hemorrhage was increased. [paper26]

**Variant notes:** Single-centre observational (paper01/02/03/05/14/26): structured headers (Rationale-and-Objectives/Background/Methods/Results/Conclusion or JAHA Background/Methods-and-Results/Conclusions); effect sizes as paired absolute rates + exact P (paper01) or %→OR→CI→P with embraced null (paper05) or adjusted OR-by-arm for benefit AND its safety counterweight (paper26); conclusion = hedged practice recommendation / independent-predictor claim / 'In this cohort ... although ...' association register; graphic abstract + Key Words common. Imaging/diagnostic-accuracy register (paper02/04/07/09/11/12/13/14): cut-off-first AUC/sens/spec/NPV, continuous predictor translated into an actionable cutoff, dual aim (technical optimization + prognostic value, paper07), European Radiology Key Points box (Question/Findings/Clinical relevance), do-not-exclude (paper11) or surrogate (paper12) directive; paper09 (prospective diagnostic-accuracy, STROBE-declared — NOT STARD) foregrounds blinded readers + DSA reference standard and leads Results with the sens/spec/accuracy triad. Landmark/secondary RCT (paper04/06/19/20/21/22/24/25): NEJM rigid BACKGROUND/METHODS/RESULTS/CONCLUSIONS with one-sentence evidence-gap Background, primary safety given equal billing, and the fixed 'but had more...' harm clause + registration parenthetical (paper21/22); Lancet Findings/Interpretation + 'Research in context' box, framing a meta-analysis as failure-to-establish-non-inferiority tied to an interpretable absolute quantity (paper23); JAMA structured headers + Key Points triad + discrete TRIAL REGISTRATION field, hedged 'suggest'/'may consider' Meaning (paper20/25); post-hoc analyses name the trial as data source ('Data from the CMOSS trial') and add an explicit small-sample/retrospective caveat + prospective-confirmation call (paper04/06).

## Introduction

**Goal:** Run the clinical funnel — disease burden / standard-of-care AND its quantified limitation → residual-risk-despite-best-care pivot → a precise clinical-evidence / patient-selection / decision gap (not a mechanistic unknown) → a dual decision-oriented aim that mirrors the title and abstract purpose verbatim in structure — keeping mechanism subordinate and immediately translated into a management consequence.

**Clinical moves:**
- Open on disease burden/prognosis (with approximation hedges ≈/up to/an estimated) or the established standard-of-care stated as settled fact ('[Therapy] has revolutionized/become a standard approach'), not on pathophysiology; quantify burden in the second breath.
- Use the 'despite-best-treatment, risk persists' move with a concrete residual-risk number (recurrence 6–11%, 'exceeding 10% annually', '~50% do not achieve mRS 0–2 at 90 days'), then pivot with a single adversative ('However,... remains unclear/uncertain/has not been well delineated; no RCT evidence').
- Build the unmet need from the evidence/guideline landscape — name landmark trials/registries by acronym (DAWN, DEFUSE 3, ATTENTION, BAOCHE, COSS, CMOSS, SAMMPRIS), credit each then name its specific shortfall, and carve the gap precisely around a patient subgroup or decision boundary (the ~30% excluded with low NIHSS; benefit only post hoc), using scarcity language softened by humility hedges ('To the best of our knowledge', 'research targeting specific groups ... remains sparse').
- For observational/imaging work, run the concession-then-gap engine ('Although [accepted benefit], it has increasingly been recognized that [inconvenient fact]'), stack-and-knock-down candidate alternatives on clinical-usability/accuracy grounds (gold standard 'not always available'; surrogate 'accuracy ... debatable'; convenient test 'highly dependent on sophisticated post-processing software'), and escalate the limitation into explicit patient-side harm ('to avoid denying treatment to patients who might still benefit').
- Subordinate mechanism: cite biology only to motivate the clinical question, chaining it to a procedural consequence (mechanism → fewer reocclusions → fewer passes), and add a patient-centred clinical-urgency rationale framed as management not biology.
- Close with a single explicit decision-oriented aim ('Thus, we conducted...', 'Accordingly, we examined...', 'the purpose of this study was to evaluate the diagnostic performance of...') pairing the scientific question with the clinical payoff, justifying the design clinically (PSM for real-world confounding) and framing novelty as generalizability/confirmation/durability rather than first-discovery.

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Opening on a molecular pathway/mechanism or device physics for its own sake instead of disease/standard-of-care burden or the patient scenario.
- Framing the gap as a knowledge-of-biology void ('poorly understood', 'understudied') rather than a clinical-evidence/patient-selection/decision deficiency at a concrete boundary; citing the gap vaguely without quantifying who is excluded and why the question is open.
- Disparaging prior trials instead of summarizing them by patient-relevant outcomes; claiming false isolation by not naming the landmark trials/registries that define the landscape.
- Stating the aim as pure mechanism elucidation or a model question with no decision payoff, or letting the aim drift from the title/abstract structure (breaking the purpose handshake).
- Over-claiming novelty as discovery rather than the bounded 'To our knowledge'/generalizability/confirmation frame.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**
- Although the link between obesity and stroke has been extensively explored in the general population, research targeting speciﬁc groups, such as patients with symptomatic ICA or MCA occlusion, remains sparse. Determining whether obesity consistently alters the risk of recurrent stroke in this demographic could help in identifying those who might be suitable candidates for revascularization procedures. [paper04]
- Despite optimal medical management, the annual incidence of recurrent ischemic stroke in patients with symptomatic ICAO and hemodynamic insufficiency can reach 6–11%. [paper03]
- given that some metrics are highly dependent on the sophisticated post-processing software, they cannot be widely applied in clinical practice. Therefore, a simple, accessible, and reproducible imaging marker with the potential to predict the prognosis of patients with AIS after EVT is urgently needed. [paper13]
- No randomised controlled trial has established a benefit of intravenous thrombolysis with alteplase in patients eligible for endovascular treatment presenting directly at endovascular treatment-capable centres. [paper23]
- It is estimated that ≈30% of patients with a symptomatic BAO have a low National Institutes of Health Stroke Scale (NIHSS score <10), but these patients were excluded from previous studies or only accounted for a small subgroup, leaving the efficacy of EVT in BAO stroke with low NIHSS scores uncertain. [paper26]

**Variant notes:** Single-centre observational (paper01/02/03/05/14/26): opener on standard-of-care limitation (paper01), complication burden + clinical-imperative bridge 'early identifying ... is critical' (paper02), disease-burden epidemiology (paper03), or a definitional patient-centred sentence + a 3-beat conflicting-evidence chain (paper05); aim comparative or association-in-a-named-cohort, paper26 carving the gap around an RCT-excluded subgroup and closing with a hedged 'explore the effectiveness'. Imaging/diagnostic-accuracy register (paper07/09/11/12/13/14): four-beat funnel (disease+natural history → reference-standard limitation as patient risk → noninvasive alternative fails for a reason that causes diagnostic error → 'to our knowledge ... not yet reported' → purpose naming the real clinical comparators, paper09), or residual-burden opener + 'convenient/simple/accessible' justification (paper12/13); paper14 runs a concession-then-gap engine ('recanalization does not necessarily lead to reperfusion') with a hedged hypothesis stated twice; physics sandwiched in the middle, never the lead. Landmark/secondary RCT (paper04/06/19/20/21/22/23/24/25): funnel built from the trial/guideline landscape with a regulatory hook where it grounds relevance (intracranial stenting 'FDA-approved exclusively for ... stroke', paper06), even-handed prior-trial summary, gap as an evidentiary/population deficiency ('no RCT evidence' at the 24-h boundary, paper19; benefit only post hoc, paper25), aim mirroring abstract Purpose verbatim ('to further test the hypothesis that endovascular therapy would be superior ... in a population different from that in previous trials', paper21); meta-analysis (paper23) funnel built on the history of clinical practice → present decision dilemma → quantified affected population → pooling justified by decision payoff.

## Methods

**Goal:** Establish clinical-epidemiology / trial-grade credibility and reproducibility while keeping every choice subordinate to the clinical message: genre-correct reporting standard + full ethics/governance apparatus, consecutive/registry/randomized provenance with a denominator funnel, guideline-anchored eligibility, endpoints operationally defined and tied to named external consensus definitions/scales (with range, direction, timepoint, cut-off), blinded/validity-aware adjudication, and a prespecified, distribution-aware statistical plan interpreted for the reader — with statistical defensiveness kept here so Results/Discussion stay clinical.

**Clinical moves:**
- State provenance precisely: single-centre = 'consecutive' patients + dated calendar window + institution type + numbered inclusion/exclusion + a denominator funnel/flowchart (screened→per-group→a-priori exclusions→analyzed); registry/trial = named acronym, dates, number of centres, exhaustive design adjectives ('investigator-initiated, prospective, open-label, multicenter, randomized, controlled ... with blinded outcome assessment'), follow-up-retention denominator; for secondary analyses inherit and delegate the parent trial's design ('detailed study design ... elaborated elsewhere').
- Declare the genre-correct reporting standard by name — STROBE for observational, CONSORT for an RCT, PRISMA-IPD/PROSPERO for pooled analysis — and note where it is candidly absent; for diagnostic-accuracy work, STARD is the normative checklist BUT the corpus never uses it: the diagnostic-accuracy exemplar (paper09) declares STROBE, NOT STARD, so do not assert STARD as observed practice. Anchor eligibility to cited practice guidelines and operational thresholds (stenosis cut-offs, French-size bore, mRS gate, time windows, rCBF<30% core / Tmax>6s).
- Define every endpoint operationally and verbatim from a named, citable external standard with its scale range/direction/timepoint and a clinical label on each cut-off (Heidelberg Bleeding Classification; mTICI/eTICI ≥2b = successful recanalization; mRS 0–2 = functional independence with the standard dichotomy; DCI per Vergouwen 2010; NASCET/WASID stenosis; sICH with the explicit NIHSS-change criterion); justify biomarker thresholds and any non-default cut-off (mRS 0–3 for BAO) by external evidence + citation; keep a separate, stricter reference-standard reading rule distinct from the index-test rule for accuracy designs and justify a multi-tier reference in decision/safety terms.
- Report reader/adjudicator rigor to diagnostic-accuracy / trial standard: named assessors + years of experience, dual independent review, senior-reviewer/tie-breaker consensus, blinding scope spelled out candidly (or 'blinding not feasible' stated honestly), reliability statistic (κ/ICC with 95% CI + a pre-stated interpretive band), washout/randomized case order to pre-empt recall/work-up bias; for trial-derived work name the independent outcome/clinical-events committee, imaging core lab, DSMB, and a registration number + data-integrity attestation.
- Pre-state the statistical plan tersely and prospectively (not defensively): distribution test first (Shapiro-Wilk → mean±SD vs median[IQR]) with named tests per variable type; the multivariable variable-entry rule (univariate P<0.10/0.15 → backward stepwise → retained at P<0.05); collinearity by named metric+threshold (VIF>10); cut-off by Youden index; head-to-head AUC by DeLong; confounding-control apparatus framed as robustness (PSM with caliper 0.2 SD / SMD≤0.1 pre-declared, IPTW 'generates a pseudo-population mimicking a randomized trial', GEE/multiple-imputation with Rubin's rules); named software+versions; two-sided α; analysis populations (ITT primary + per-protocol/safety support) and missing-data policy; subgroups prespecified as effect-modification checks not data-dredging.
- Give the full ethics/governance stanza in house order (protocol availability, Declaration of Helsinki, named IRB + approval number, written consent including proxies OR a design-justified retrospective consent waiver, deidentification, registration, HIPAA-non-applicability where relevant); disclose any prespecified contingency (proportional-odds violation → Brant test → binary/generalized-OR primary), interim-look alpha spending, and any honest journal-requested method-switch non-defensively; for prospective designs give an a priori, clinically framed sample-size calculation on a named primary endpoint.

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Naming the wrong reporting standard (STROBE/STARD for an RCT) or none; ASSERTING STARD as observed practice for a diagnostic-accuracy paper when the corpus declares STROBE and never uses STARD; omitting the denominator funnel/attrition accounting so screened→analyzed cannot be reconstructed.
- Defining endpoints ad hoc / by investigator opinion instead of a cited consensus definition with explicit cut-offs/time windows, or using a scale without its range, direction, and timepoint; conflating the index-test rule with the reference-standard rule.
- Omitting reader experience/blinding/adjudication or reporting accuracy without a reliability statistic (κ/ICC) and its interpretive band; reporting safety casually while efficacy is rigorously defined.
- Over-explaining/defending model internals in reviewer-response tone (EPP, convergence, VIF rubrics, equations) instead of a terse pre-specified plan — push such detail to the Supplement; judging post-match balance by p-values instead of a pre-declared SMD/caliper; switching the primary outcome post hoc rather than disclosing a prespecified contingency.
- Stating tests generically without matching them to variable distribution/type, omitting software versions/two-sidedness/analysis-population/missing-data policy, or forgetting the consent-waiver justification, the ethics/registration block, or the genre-appropriate reporting checklist.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**
- From January 2019 to January 2024, a total of 4752 patients underwent large-bore neurointerventions (> 6 F) at our center, comprising 89 cases of TBA, 176 cases of TRA, and 4487 cases of TFA. To minimize potential biases, the procedures with fewer than five cases of neurointervention via a single access route were excluded from the study. [paper01]
- Successful recanalization was defined as grade ≥2b of the modified Thrombolysis in Cerebral Infarction score. Two neuroradiologists who were blinded to the patient's medical information, including laboratory characteristics, independently evaluated all imaging findings. For any divergence between the two assessors, another experienced neuroradiologist reassessed the neuroimages and helped to reach a final consensus. [paper02]
- Variables with a p < 0.1 in the univariate analysis were entered into the multivariate logistic regression analysis to identify the independent variables associated with GIC. Collinearity analysis was performed using a variance inflation factor (VIF) > 10 as the threshold value [22]. [paper11]
- For the primary efficacy outcome, the proportional-odds assumption for the ordinal logistic-regression model was not satisfied, and therefore the Wilcoxon–Mann–Whitney generalized odds ratio and 95% confidence interval were calculated in an assumption-free ordinal analysis to detect a shift in the distribution of scores on the modified Rankin scale. There were no missing data in the primary outcome analysis. [paper21]
- The study adhered to the STROBE (Strengthening the Reporting of Observational Studies in Epidemiology) statement. The study protocol was approved by the ethics committees of Sun Yat-sen Memorial Hospital, Sun Yat-sen University (SYSKY-2024-602-01), and all participating centers. Informed consent from the patients was waived due to the retrospective design. [paper26]

**Variant notes:** Single-centre observational (paper01/02/03/05/14/26): denominator funnel + consecutive/dated/institution descriptor + CONSORT-style flowchart; operator pool/experience quantified for procedural studies (paper01); endpoints split into primary vs safety with operational definitions; PSM details pre-stated (logit, 1:4 or 1:1 greedy nearest-neighbor, caliper 0.2 SD, exposure force-retained); STROBE declared in the registry-style multicentre observational (paper05/26) but NOT in the earliest single-centre set (paper01/02/03, which lean on named clinical standards + journal templates); heavy confounding-control apparatus (MICE, PSM, IPTW, PS-adjustment) framed as robustness (paper26). Imaging/diagnostic-accuracy (paper07/09/11/12/13/14): blinded dual-reader + senior-adjudicator consensus with κ/ICC + interpretive band, ROC/Youden cutoff and DeLong AUC comparison, DWI as a named reference standard with ordinal grading (paper07/12); paper09 (prospective) adds STARD-genre diagnostic-accuracy machinery — a stricter DSA-specific reference rule, a justified 2-tier reference standard, unit-of-analysis cluster-bootstrap CIs for stents-within-patient, an a priori power calc, 4-week washout/randomized order — but DECLARES STROBE, not STARD (STARD appears nowhere in the corpus and must not be asserted as observed practice; if it is named at all it is only a normative recommendation the papers do not follow); a self-label tension surfaces (European Radiology Methodology box says 'case-control' for a single-arm outcome-prediction cohort, paper11/12/13). Landmark/secondary RCT (paper04/06/19/20/21/22/23/24/25): exhaustive governance stanza (steering committee, IRB, DSMB, CRO, blinded adjudication/clinical-events committee, AE committee, core lab, independent statistician), CONSORT cited, ITT primary + per-protocol support, sample size grounded in external evidence stated clinically, honest disclosures (failed proportional-odds → generalized OR, O'Brien–Fleming alpha spending, journal-requested OR→RR change, method-switch-at-zero-events to risk difference); post-hoc analyses inherit the parent trial verbatim and signal validity via blinded independent committee + imaging core lab (paper06/20); a secondary-analysis-of-an-RCT may correctly cite STROBE (not CONSORT) for the observational extension (paper20); meta-analysis (paper23) cites PRISMA-IPD/PROSPERO, justifies design by clinical rationale, derives the non-inferiority margin from a survey+guidelines, uses ICEMAN for subgroup credibility and within-trial-effects-first to avoid ecological bias, delegating ethics/consent to parent trials.

## Results

**Goal:** Report a patient-centred narrative where the main text states the clinical conclusion and display items hold the exact numbers: an auditable screening-to-inclusion funnel, quantified matching adequacy, the universal n/N(%) reporting unit, every between-group effect as a single inferential unit (comparator + direction + paired values + named data-type-matched effect estimate + 95% CI, P reserved for the prespecified primary), accuracy reported rule-out-first/cut-off-first, safety paired side-by-side with benefit, and nulls/harms/subgroups/zero-event cells handled transparently.

**Clinical moves:**
- Open with exhaustive, decision-centred cohort accounting (screening funnel with running counts + itemized exclusion reasons, CONSORT/PRISMA-style flow, crossovers/withdrawals/missing-primary reconciled patient-by-patient) and a Table-1-mirroring cohort one-liner (median[IQR], n[%]); for prospective designs add a procedural-safety statement.
- Quantify matching adequacy rather than asserting it (SMD, variance ratio, before/after balance) and translate the verdict into clinical reassurance; carry every rate as numerator AND denominator + % (group-specific denominators repeated each time, re-stated transparently when the analysis population changes, per-variable n printed for missing data) — never a bare percentage.
- Write each between-group outcome as a single inferential unit: name the comparator ('Compared to TFA'), state direction/clinical meaning, give the paired raw values, then the effect estimate matched to the data type (OR/aOR/HR/rate ratio/generalized OR/RR/β; absolute risk difference for trials/registries) with 95% CI — placing the clinical-direction adjective BEFORE the number and using 'independently associated with'/'in favor of' as the connective; show crude AND adjusted for the primary and interpret the gap as confounding control; let CIs/absolute-rate contrasts (not bare p-values) carry interpretation.
- For accuracy: report the full 2×2-derived panel cut-off-first (decision rule before the statistic), each metric paired to its raw fraction, foregrounding NPV/specificity (rule-out) then AUC, with reader agreement (κ/ICC + plain-language grade) and any incremental/head-to-head model value significance-tested (ΔAUC with P, or non-inferiority with the explicit null 'no significant difference in AUC ... p=0.686'); then dichotomize patients into actionable groups.
- Report procedural/process metrics (recanalization, FPE, mTICI) as medians(IQR)/proportions with denominators benchmarked to literature; pair efficacy with the named safety axis (HT/sICH with graded subtype counts) for interventional questions, on the absolute risk-difference scale right alongside benefit.
- Treat nulls and harms honestly: nulls as 'no evidence of a difference' (not 'no difference'), harms with the SAME rigor as benefit (report wide CIs on rare events rather than suppressing them; mark zero-event cells non-estimable with an ellipsis while still reporting P), non-significant directional signals as 'numerically more common'/'trend' shown with numbers but not spun; show robustness as a concordant stack of sensitivity/per-protocol/matched re-analyses ('were similar to', 'yielded results consistent with').
- Present subgroups with restraint as a forest plot emphasizing directional consistency with per-stratum adjusted estimate + P-for-interaction (and an explicit underpowered/multiplicity caveat), not subgroup-hunting; narrate marquee numerator events case-by-case with mechanism + resolution, bridge a key discordant result to its clinical concept ('indicating futile recanalization'), and call figures/tables with content-describing active verbs + terse end-anchors, offloading dense baseline/full-model/robustness tables to supplements.

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Reporting a bare P or a bare percentage without paired absolute values / numerator-denominator, hiding the small event counts driving wide CIs; failing to re-state a shifting denominator when the analysis population changes.
- Reporting an effect estimate without naming the measure that produced it or matching it to the data type; scattering P-values across secondary/subgroup outcomes instead of letting CIs do the interpretive work; reporting only adjusted (or only crude) for the primary instead of both.
- Spinning a non-significant 'trend' as a real effect, writing 'no difference' for a null, suppressing a wide non-significant CI on a rare harm, or fabricating an OR for a zero-event cell instead of marking it non-estimable.
- Leading the accuracy paragraph with AUC alone instead of rule-out metrics + the actionable cutoff; reporting percentages without raw fractions/denominators, breaking the diagnostic-accuracy signature.
- Asserting 'groups were well-balanced' without an SMD/variance-ratio number; mining subgroup interactions for significance without P-for-interaction and a multiplicity hedge; dumping the full model/dense baseline/robustness apparatus into the main text instead of offloading to supplements.

The effect spine differs by genre (absolute units / OR-HR for observational; HR or generalized-OR shift for RCTs; κ/r/group-means/AUC for imaging) but all use n/N(%) and transparent denominators.

## Discussion

**Goal:** Open by restating the design + population + headline patient finding in plain clinical/outcome terms before any mechanism; situate it in a clinical evidence map using identical statistical grammar to named comparators (concordance/discordance as the connective backbone, discrepancies explained clinically); translate effect sizes into bedside-intuitive currency (X-fold, one-in-five, NNT, natural frequencies); contain mechanism to a single subordinated/hedged paragraph offered only as biological plausibility; and land repeatedly on an operator-/decision-facing clinical-implications paragraph that names the bedside actor and the patient-selection / benefit-risk weighing, with hedging calibrated to the statistics.

**Clinical moves:**
- Open with a one-sentence restatement of the principal finding in patient-population + outcome terms (and for predictor work, note independence from confounders + give the actionable threshold) before any interpretation; then address safety.
- Position against prior work by name (credit each, then name its limit/contrast), importing comparator estimates in the SAME point-estimate+CI+P grammar, using concordance connectors ('In line with', 'Consistent with', 'which was also confirmed in the present study') and a credit-then-differentiate move ('[Author] et al first reported...; Unlike these studies, we...'); make the discrepancy the argument and explain it clinically (race/case-mix, treatment-modality, occlusion-vs-stenosis mechanism) rather than statistically.
- Translate effect estimates into intuitive language and bedside quantities (a clinical-significance stamp for biomarkers; NNT with its own CI named to a clinical action, including an NNT trajectory across timepoints; absolute risk difference; per-hour time cost; 'Nearly 30% ... compared with 10%') and benchmark against established therapies/registries with the comparator's own numbers quoted.
- Make the clinical-implications landing explicit and signposted ('These findings have significant clinical implications.', 'Our findings have important clinical significance', 'A critical question arises: which patients...?'), naming the bedside actor (clinician, patient, caregiver) and concrete decision uses (surveillance intervals, antiplatelet adherence, integrating a threshold into a workflow, do-not-exclude rule, shared decision-making, cost-effectiveness), subordinating mechanism (means) to the endpoint (point) with 'More importantly,...'.
- Address the chief clinical worry head-on and reframe it as manageable (complication concern → learning-curve/training; harm in mild strokes → lower tolerance for HT); elevate safety with a stock transition ('Beyond efficacy, safety remains a top priority'), normalize harm against the literature, and convert it into actionable selection advice; dismiss competing tools on workflow/feasibility/turnaround/cost grounds, not biology.
- Keep mechanism contained to one hedged plausibility paragraph ('may reflect', 'presumably because', 'As for the potential reason, we deemed that...', 'might be attributed', 'only partly explains'), used only to explain who-benefits/why-a-harm-matters; reconcile discordant trials via clinically meaningful design/population differences ('justified by'); state the evidentiary ceiling explicitly ('not powered to draw conclusions on...'), downgrade underpowered secondary/subgroup data to 'hypothesis generating', and maintain explicit association-not-causation discipline with a design-tagged disclaimer.
- Land on the clinical decision with population-scoped, hedged, conditional recommendations gated on future confirmation, each paired with a research call (often converting a subgroup signal into a targeted next-trial recommendation while responsibly flagging potential harm in the non-benefiting subgroup).

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Leading the Discussion with mechanism/pathophysiology, a statistical recap, or a literature review instead of the restated clinical finding in population terms.
- Citing comparators in a different statistical idiom so concordance cannot be judged; explaining a discrepancy purely statistically when a clinical explanation (case-mix, modality, race) is available; claiming isolation instead of benchmarking with concrete numbers.
- Leaving effect sizes as bare statistics without NNT / absolute-difference / benchmark translation, or making unhedged causal/treatment-mandate claims from observational/post-hoc designs without the association-not-causation disclaimer.
- Letting mechanistic speculation expand beyond a single contained, hedged paragraph or presenting it as the contribution/headline; dismissing a competing tool on biological grounds when the differentiator is workflow/turnaround/cost.
- Over-interpreting a null or underpowered wide-CI estimate as proof of equivalence; over-claiming subgroup/secondary findings instead of downgrading to hypothesis-generating and flagging multiplicity; failing to translate the finding into a concrete bedside action or omitting the evidentiary-ceiling statement.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**
- In contrast, D-­dimer, which is reported within half an hour and requires no additional calculations, is more suitable for use in emergencies. [paper02]
- It is important to note that our study, being a post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial with a limited sample size and a low event rate, suggests only an association between BMI levels and the incidence of IIS in medically treated patients with symptomatic artery occlusion, rather than deﬁnitively establishing a causal relationship. [paper04]
- Patients should not be excluded from recanalization therapy solely based on the ischemic core volume obtained from baseline CTP, especially in the early time window. [paper11]
- Together, the 2 trials generate a strong hypothesis that adjunctive tirofiban treatment should target patients with large vessel occlusion who have had acute ischemic stroke due to intracranial atherosclerotic disease and merit a future confirmatory phase 3 trial confined to this population. **Variant notes:** Single-centre observational (paper01/02/03/05/14/26): opener restates headline in outcome terms (paper01) or re-grounds in the technical-success-vs-functional-outcome paradox (paper02); concordance/discordance the recurring connective, discrepant rates explained clinically (limited sample, race uniformity, treatment-method difference, paper05); confounding-by-indication made explicit (crude-vs-adjusted gap = control of negative confounding, paper26) often with a guideline/survey/missing-evidence triad; mechanism quarantined to one hedged paragraph; competing tools dismissed on workflow grounds; concrete surveillance/antiplatelet/measurement-validity recommendations (every-3-month ultrasound, paper03; validate pc-NIHSS, paper26). Imaging/diagnostic-accuracy (paper07/09/11/12/13/14): opens around clinical deliverables, brackets physics with clinical-task framing, owns trade-offs (SNR/CNR, paper09), gives hedged mechanism only to justify why salvageable tissue is mislabelled (paper11) or why a measurement is advantageous (paper12), calibrates use to a favourable subgroup with a discordance caveat, lands via 'Taken together' on a surveillance/risk-stratification/bedside-action role ('a reinforced medication or rehabilitation therapy should be initiated as soon as possible', paper13). Landmark/secondary RCT (paper04/06/19/20/21/22/23/24/25): the signature move is NNT-with-its-own-CI translation + benchmark-against-established-therapy (paper22) and the strongest NNT/natural-frequency translation with trajectory verbs (sustained/magnified, NNT 1-in-8→1-in-5, paper20) — WARNING: a fully composed NNT-8.2-with-CI sentence is a SYNTHETIC TEMPLATE for this move, NOT a verified verbatim quote; do not present it as an exemplar quote until verified against the source paper22 Discussion. ## Limitations

**Goal:** Acknowledge constraints with a canonical interpret-with-caution opener, then enumerate them ordinally (First/Second/.../Finally), each naming the specific bias TYPE AND its likely DIRECTION/consequence on the estimate or on generalizability — framed around clinical generalizability / tool readiness / who-this-applies-to rather than abstract statistical confession — pairing each weakness with a mitigation, directional reassurance, or strength where honest, converting it into a concrete future-research/validate call, and placing generalizability (grounded in a concrete, cited population feature) as the final, most consequential caveat.

**Clinical moves:**
- Open with a canonical caution tied to the design ('single-center retrospective design, demanding the interpretation of the findings with caution'; 'should be interpreted with caution'; 'This trial has limitations.') and an ordinal scaffold (First/Second/Third/Finally or Firstly/Secondly...).
- Name each bias type explicitly (selection bias, residual confounding, partial-verification/work-up bias, crossover misclassification, single-demographic generalizability, multiplicity → type I/II error) AND state the direction it likely pushed the estimate or the specific metric it could affect ('could potentially inflate estimates of specificity and NPV'; 'diluting the estimated treatment effect and underestimating the true benefit'; 'may have disadvantaged the medical-management group'; 'the efficacy of EVT may be overestimated').
- Pair each limitation with a mitigation/strength/directional reassurance via pivots ('Fortunately', 'Nevertheless,', 'despite utilizing the largest ... dataset', 'However, our study used PSM ... these are strengths') or pre-empt a sophisticated method critique then defend with the paper's own data (King & Nielsen on PSM; over-matching paradox; concordant IPTW+IVA; pessimistic-scenario sensitivity analysis).
- Frame around clinical generalizability and tool readiness (small event n weakening power — with the a priori power figure where honest; no external validation; single-ethnicity cohort; short follow-up; retrospective exclusion of named variables; selection of the sickest excluded because they could not tolerate MRI; high-volume experienced centres) rather than generic statistical defensiveness; engage domain nuance where relevant (Asian-vs-WHO BMI cut-offs; NIHSS underweighting posterior-circulation severity; ATTENTION/BAOCHE NIHSS thresholds).
- Acknowledge multiplicity explicitly as a type I error risk with a caution directive and acknowledge unblinding candidly when present, then offer a constructive remedy (independent outcome assessment committee, longer follow-up); name ongoing/planned confirmatory trials by NCT number as forward-pointing devices.
- Close forward-looking, placing generalizability as the final, most consequential limitation grounded in a concrete cited population-specific feature (Chinese cohort with high intracranial-atherosclerosis prevalence; single-modality cohort) and converting each weakness into a specific evidence-level-upgrade call (validate / verify accuracy / prospective RCT / multicentre trial across hospital levels).

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Listing limitations generically without naming the bias type, its mechanism, or the direction/consequence on the estimate.
- Omitting the mitigation/strength/directional reassurance where an honest one exists, or treating residual confounding after PSM as fully resolved.
- Treating limitations as statistical confession rather than clinical-generalizability/tool-readiness/who-this-applies-to framing; claiming broad generalizability while the cohort is single-demographic/single-modality.
- Omitting the multiplicity caveat for subgroup analyses or failing to acknowledge unblinding when relevant; generic 'results may not be generalizable' without grounding in a concrete cited population feature.
- Failing to convert weaknesses into concrete future-research calls, or burying generalizability mid-list instead of placing it as the final, most consequential caveat.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**
- First, despite utilizing the largest single-­center dataset on patients undergoing recanalization treatment for non-­acute ICAO, the sample size of cases with restenosis was relatively small. This limitation may have masked potentially relevant factors and diminished the predictive power of our scoring system. [paper03]
- Finally, not all patients underwent DSA confirmation. However, DSA was performed in all patients with a high suspicion of ISR. In patients without suspected ISR—particularly asymptomatic individuals—DSA was not routinely performed to avoid unnecessary invasive procedures. - The primary limitation of ATTENTION is its observational nature, as residual confounding from unmeasured risk factors remains a concern that only a randomized controlled design can fully address. ## Conclusion

**Goal:** Mirror the abstract conclusion almost verbatim with a tight, decision-centred, population-scoped close — restate the strongest finding firmly (often a memorable headline or directional verdict per outcome tier) then pivot to a hedged, decision-actor-oriented clinical offer; for trials a balanced benefit-with-harm verdict, for observational/imaging a finding-then-hedged-role pivot; no new content, no mechanism, no model-performance recap, calibrated to the evidence grade and ending on patient selection plus a confirmation-needed coda where design warrants.

**Clinical moves:**
- Mirror the abstract: reuse the headline framing (memorable risk statement, named predictor triad, surrogate/prognostic role, first-line-choice recommendation) and the design tag ('In conclusion', 'In this post-hoc analysis of the CMOSS trial', 'In this long-term follow-up study among randomized patients...'), keeping the close consistent with the Abstract conclusion and the final Discussion paragraph.
- Restate the strongest data claim firmly (often carrying the signature tension phrase, e.g. 'despite successful recanalization', or a directional verdict per outcome tier with mRS bands named), then pivot to a hedged clinical-value statement; use a two-tier or pattern-derived landing where applicable (a safety/feasibility/risk statement, then a discriminating practice recommendation derived from the pattern of contrasts).
- For trials/registries, deliver the balanced benefit-risk verdict in a single bedside-quotable sentence retaining harm with 'albeit'/'but had more...'/a three-part benefit→harm→mortality-reassurance ledger, scoping therapeutic potential to 'carefully selected'/'selected patients' via the guidance template 'Among patients with [phenotype], [intervention] resulted in [patient outcome] ...'; bookend on the decision actor and shared decision-making ('These results may assist clinicians and patients in the decision-making process').
- Keep the closing word on patient selection / management decision and reaffirm the surrogate-to-hard-outcome link where the paper established one; hedge in proportion to the evidence ('may favor', 'may identify', 'may add', 'might be a potential surrogate', 'is associated with' for observational), label observational findings 'hypothesis-generating' where warranted, and append a measured confirmation call ('Further studies are warranted', 'Future larger prospective cohort studies are needed', 'Randomized controlled trials are therefore needed') for observational/post-hoc designs.
- For a null/non-inferiority result, close on a practice rule (often a prioritisation-verb triad, 'should not prohibit, delay, or be prioritised over') plus an individualised-decision/future-research pathway rather than a verdict, optionally reframing a procedural endpoint toward its clinically meaningful attribute ('first-pass recanalization' → 'rapid recanalization'); drop mechanism and model-performance internals entirely.

**Basic-science pitfalls:**
- Introducing new claims, data, mechanism, or model-performance recap not in the abstract; or letting the conclusion drift from the exact eligible population specified in the abstract.
- Stopping at 'feasible'/'associated' without the discriminating decision statement when the data support one, or stating the firm finding without the hedged clinical-value pivot.
- Over-claiming with a treatment mandate ('support the use of') or causal/practice-change language inappropriate to the evidence grade (must be softened to 'support the consideration of ... in carefully selected patients', and softened further for observational work).
- Stating benefit without its paired harm clause (trials) or dropping the mortality/benefit-risk ledger; failing to label an observational finding 'hypothesis-generating' or to scope a recommendation to 'selected patients'.
- Dropping the confirmation-needed coda for an observational/post-hoc design, ending on a generic 'further research is warranted' tail instead of a clinical decision message, or closing on mechanism/statistics rather than the patient outcome and decision actor.

**Exemplars (verbatim):**

- Post-treatment DWI ASPECTS might be a potential surrogate of infarction volume in terms of providing the prognostic information for AIS patients after EVT. [paper12]
- Given the study's nonrandomized design, missing data, center-level variability, and potential for residual confounding, these findings should be considered hypothesis-generating. Randomized controlled trials are therefore needed to determine whether EVT truly confers a net clinical benefit in this late-presenting population and to guide future treatment recommendations. [paper19]
- These findings support the consideration of endovascular therapy in carefully selected patients with medium-vessel occlusion. [paper22]
- Our findings suggest that EVT is associated with an increased likelihood of 90-day functional independence in acute BAO stroke with an NIHSS score of <10 within 24 hours of onset, compared with SMT alone. EVT could increase the risk of symptomatic intracerebral hemorrhage, but this does not contribute to a higher mortality. The findings should be further validated in randomized trials in the future. [paper26]
