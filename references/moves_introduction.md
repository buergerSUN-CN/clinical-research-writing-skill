# Introduction moves

Build a short clinical funnel: known burden or standard care → unresolved decision → exact aim.

## Open on the clinical state {#intro-clinical-state}

- Decision: begin with disease burden, current standard care, treatment limitation, diagnostic gap, or
  residual poor outcome after technically successful care, but only when the supplied manuscript or
  cited evidence supports that premise.
- Evidence: dominant opener families across all 32 exemplars.
- Failure mode: opening on a signaling pathway, device engineering detail, or generic global-burden
  sentence unrelated to the study decision; also reject plausible but uncited background invented to
  complete a sparse source.

## Use hard evidence, not a hook {#intro-hard-evidence}

- Decision: attach specific burden or outcome magnitudes to their citations when the source supports
  them.
- Evidence: common in observational and imaging introductions.
- Failure mode: rhetorical fractions, `devastating burden`, or uncited magnitude inflation.

## Name relevant prior evidence {#intro-prior-evidence}

- Decision: summarize the studies, trials, registries, or guideline position that define the current
  uncertainty; include their decisive result rather than listing citations.
- Evidence: trial acronyms were prominent in trial-family papers; cohort size and setting were more
  useful in observational/imaging comparisons.
- Failure mode: citation chains with no indication of what each source established.

## State a falsifiable gap {#intro-gap}

- Decision: identify what is unknown about the population, endpoint, test, comparator, external
  validity, or management consequence.
- Evidence: all families.
- Failure mode: `remains incompletely understood` when the manuscript does not specify what is missing.
- Source boundary: if the source provides only an objective and no evidence review, state the objective
  without inventing a literature gap.

## Anchor the aim to the study {#intro-aim}

- Decision: name the actual cohort, registry, trial, center structure, or dataset and the primary
  clinical question.
- Evidence: especially strong in observational and trial-secondary papers.
- Failure mode: an abstract scientific aim that could describe a different dataset.

## Keep mechanism subordinate {#intro-mechanism}

- Decision: include only the mechanistic rationale needed to explain why the exposure or test could be
  clinically informative.
- Evidence: the corpus prioritized patient outcomes and management over extended biological review.
- Failure mode: a mechanistic mini-review that delays the clinical uncertainty.
